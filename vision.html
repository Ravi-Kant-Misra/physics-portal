Teaching Philosophy

My Vision

What Physics Foundation is really about

Why This Class Exists

Physics Foundation exists for one reason: to give students the thinking tools to understand the physical world — not just pass a test.

Most students arrive believing that physics is a collection of formulas to be memorised. They leave this class knowing it is something else entirely: a way of asking precise questions about reality, and a method for answering them systematically.

What Success Looks Like

Success in this class is not a grade.

It is the moment a student looks at a problem they have never seen before and says — I know how to approach this.

It is the student who, when asked why an object accelerates, does not recite Newton's Second Law but instead explains it in their own words, with a diagram, with an example from their own life.

It is the student who is comfortable saying "I don't understand yet" — because they know that not understanding yet is the starting point of all real learning.

Why We Go Step by Step

Every unit in this course is a building block. Significant figures before measurements. Vectors before forces. Kinematics before dynamics. This is not arbitrary — it is the order in which the concepts depend on each other.

Rushing this sequence produces students who can solve problems they have seen before but collapse when they see something new. We do not rush here.

What I Believe About Every Student

Every student in this class is capable of understanding physics at a deep level. Intelligence is not fixed. Ability in physics is not a gift you either have or don't have. It is the result of consistent effort, good questions, and a willingness to be confused for long enough to break through.

My job is to structure the learning so that breakthrough is inevitable — if you do the work.

The Standard We Hold

We hold each other to a high standard — not because perfection is expected, but because settling for surface-level understanding wastes everyone's time, including yours.

Understand it. Show it. Move forward.

— Ravi M.
