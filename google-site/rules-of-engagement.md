# RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

## Page Header
**Title:** Rules of Engagement
**Subtitle:** How we work together in Physics Foundation

## Opening paragraph
This class works because everyone in it commits to the same standard — not just getting the right answer, but understanding why it is right. These rules are not restrictions. They are the conditions that make real learning possible.

---

## The Rules

### 1. Understand Before You Write
Before picking up your pen, read the question. Think about the concept. Ask yourself: what is really being asked here? A wrong answer that shows clear thinking is worth more than a correct answer copied without understanding.

### 2. Show Every Step
Physics is a chain of reasoning. A final answer with no working is not physics — it is guessing. Show your units, your substitutions, your reasoning. If your answer is wrong, I can only help you if I can see your thinking.

### 3. Ask When You Are Stuck — Not After
The moment you feel lost is the moment to speak up — not at the end of the lesson, not the night before a test. There are no silly questions in this class. Confusion is the beginning of understanding.

### 4. Complete Each Unit Before Moving On
This course is sequential. Every concept builds on the one before it. Do not skip ahead. Do not rush. A shaky foundation makes every wall that follows unstable.

### 5. Homework is Your Proof of Understanding
Homework is not busywork. It is your chance to show — to yourself, your parent, and your teacher — that you have genuinely understood the lesson. It is reviewed by your parent before the next chapter unlocks.

### 6. Consistent Effort Beats Last-Minute Cramming
Physics rewards students who work a little every day. Students who disappear for two weeks and then panic learn the same wrong habits every time. Show up. Do the work. Trust the process.

### 7. Respect the Learning Environment
When someone is explaining — teacher or student — everyone listens. Questions are welcome. Side conversations are not.

---

## Closing note
These rules apply equally to everyone — including me. I commit to explaining clearly, giving you feedback that is useful, and never moving on until the concept is solid.

Let's build something real together.
