✅ Answer Key — Classwork

Part A — Area Calculations

A1 Section 1: ΔV = +4 × 5 = +20 ms⁻¹. Section 2: ΔV = −2 × 3 = −6 ms⁻¹.
Total ΔV = +20 + (−6) = +14 ms⁻¹
A2 Section 1: ΔV = +6 × 2 = +12 ms⁻¹. Section 2: ΔV = 0 × 3 = 0. Section 3: ΔV = −3 × 2 = −6 ms⁻¹.
Total ΔV = 12 + 0 − 6 = +6 ms⁻¹
A3 ΔV₁ = +2 × 4 = +8 ms⁻¹. ΔV₂ = −5 × 2 = −10 ms⁻¹. Total ΔV = +8 − 10 = −2 ms⁻¹. Starting at 10 ms⁻¹: v = 10 + (−2) = 8 ms⁻¹
A4 ΔV at 2s: +5×2 = +10 → v = 10 ms⁻¹
ΔV at 5s: +2×3 = +6 → v = 16 ms⁻¹
ΔV 5–7s: 0 → v = 16. ΔV 7–9s: −4×2 = −8 → v at 9s = 8 ms⁻¹
A5 u = 0, a = +3 ms⁻², t = 4 s. s = ut + ½at² = 0 + ½ × 3 × 16 = 24 m.
Displacement = 24 m
A6 Start v = 5 ms⁻¹. ΔV₁ = +10×3 = +30 → v = 35. ΔV₂ = −5×4 = −20 → v = 15. ΔV₃ = 0 → v = 15.
v at t = 10 s = 15 ms⁻¹

Part B — Conversions

B1 a₁ = (8−0)/4 = +2 ms⁻². a₂ = 0/3 = 0 ms⁻². a₃ = (2−8)/3 = −2 ms⁻². A-T graph: horizontal line at +2 (0–4s), on x-axis (4–7s), line at −2 (7–10s).
B2 0→3s: ΔV = +12, v = 12.   3→8s: ΔV = −10, v = 2.   8→10s: ΔV = 0, v = 2.
V-T graph: rises steeply to 12 ms⁻¹ at t=3s, falls to 2 ms⁻¹ at t=8s, flat at 2 ms⁻¹ to t=10s.
B3 a₁ = 15/5 = +3 ms⁻². a₂ = (−5−15)/5 = −20/5 = −4 ms⁻². a₃ = 0/4 = 0 ms⁻².
B4 v₀ = 6. ΔV₁ = −3×4 = −12 → v at 4s = −6 ms⁻¹. ΔV₂ = +5×2 = +10 → v at 6s = 4 ms⁻¹. ΔV₃ = −2×3 = −6 → v at 9s = −2 ms⁻¹.

Part C — Short Answer

C1 A horizontal line means the acceleration value stays the same for the entire time interval — it does not increase or decrease. This is the definition of constant acceleration.
C2 The student is incorrect. The area under an A-T graph equals the change in velocity (ΔV), not displacement. Displacement is the area under a V-T graph.
C3 Negative area means ΔV is negative — the object is decelerating (losing speed in the positive direction) or accelerating in the negative direction. Example: a car braking to a stop.
