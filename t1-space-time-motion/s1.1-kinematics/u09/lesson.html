Acceleration-Time Graphs

Reading, interpreting and converting A-T graphs

1 What is an Acceleration-Time Graph?
Key Facts
  • An A-T graph plots acceleration on the y-axis (units: ms⁻²) against time on the x-axis (units: s).
  • It turns complicated motion into a simple picture that is easy to read at a glance.
  • For constant acceleration, the graph is always a horizontal line — because the acceleration does not change with time.
  • A positive y-value means the object is speeding up (in the positive direction); a negative y-value means it is slowing down or accelerating backwards.

Notice: every section is a horizontal line because acceleration is constant in each segment. The line height tells you the value of acceleration; positive lines sit above the x-axis, negative lines below it.

2 Area Under an A-T Graph = Change in Velocity
The Golden Rule
  • The "area under the curve" is the rectangular area between the graph line and the x-axis.
  • Area = a × t = ΔV — this gives the change in velocity during that time interval.
  • Area above the x-axis → positive ΔV (object gains speed in the positive direction).
  • Area below the x-axis → negative ΔV (object loses speed or gains speed in the negative direction).
  • Add all ΔV values to find the total change in velocity over the whole journey.

4 Converting Velocity-Time Graphs to Acceleration-Time Graphs
The Rule
  • The slope (gradient) of a V-T graph equals the acceleration at that moment.
  • Find the slope of each straight section: slope = rise ÷ run = Δv ÷ Δt.
  • Each slope becomes a horizontal line on the corresponding A-T graph at that acceleration value.

5 Converting Acceleration-Time Graphs to Velocity-Time Graphs
Two Methods
  • Method 1 (Slope): Acceleration = slope of V-T graph. Draw V-T line segments with gradients equal to the acceleration values shown on the A-T graph.
  • Method 2 (Area): Area under A-T curve = ΔV. Starting from the initial velocity, add each ΔV to find the velocity at the end of each section, then connect with straight lines.
