Acceleration-Time Graphs

Complete all sections independently. Show full working for Part A. Due next lesson.

Reference Formulae
ΔV = a × t
Area under A-T graph
a = ΔV / Δt
Slope of V-T graph
v = u + at
Velocity equation
s = ut + ½at²
Displacement equation
💡
Homework Tip: For every A-T graph section, the area is simply a rectangle (base × height). Add areas above the x-axis as positive ΔV and areas below as negative ΔV. To get the velocity at any point, start with the initial velocity and add all the ΔV values up to that point.
Part A Calculation Problems (Show full working for each)
Question A1 [3 marks]

An A-T graph shows a = +5 ms⁻² from t = 0 to t = 4 s, then a = −3 ms⁻² from t = 4 s to t = 8 s. Calculate the total change in velocity over 8 seconds.

Question A2 [3 marks]

An object begins at rest. Its A-T graph shows: a = +8 ms⁻² (t = 0–2 s); a = 0 (t = 2–6 s); a = −4 ms⁻² (t = 6–10 s). Find the velocity at t = 2 s, t = 6 s, and t = 10 s.

Question A3 [4 marks]

An object has initial velocity u = 12 ms⁻¹. Its A-T graph shows four sections:

  • a = −2 ms⁻² from t = 0 to t = 3 s
  • a = +4 ms⁻² from t = 3 to t = 5 s
  • a = 0 from t = 5 to t = 8 s
  • a = −6 ms⁻² from t = 8 to t = 10 s
Question A4 [3 marks]

A V-T graph shows velocity increasing from 0 ms⁻¹ to 20 ms⁻¹ in 5 s, staying constant for 4 s, then decreasing to −4 ms⁻¹ over the next 6 s. Determine the acceleration in each of the three sections.

Question A5 [3 marks]

An A-T graph shows a = +7 ms⁻² from t = 0 to t = 3 s; a = −7 ms⁻² from t = 3 to t = 6 s. An object starts at v = 4 ms⁻¹.

  1. Find the velocity at t = 3 s.
  2. Find the velocity at t = 6 s.
  3. What is the net change in velocity from t = 0 to t = 6 s? What does this tell you?

Part B True or False

Circle or select True or False for each statement. If False, briefly explain why.

B1. On an acceleration-time graph, a line on the x-axis (a = 0) means the object is stationary.

B2. The area under an A-T graph for a section where a = −4 ms⁻² over 3 s gives a ΔV of −12 ms⁻¹.

B3. If the slope of a V-T graph is zero, the corresponding A-T graph shows a horizontal line at a non-zero value.

B4. If the total area under an A-T graph (combining positive and negative sections) is zero, the object's velocity is the same at the end as at the beginning.

Part C Challenge Questions
Challenge C1 [5 marks]

A cyclist starts from rest. The A-T graph of their ride shows:

  • a = +2 ms⁻² from t = 0 to t = 5 s (acceleration phase)
  • a = 0 from t = 5 to t = 15 s (constant speed)
  • a = −2.5 ms⁻² from t = 15 to t = 19 s (braking)

(a) What is the maximum speed reached?
(b) What is the final speed?
(c) During the braking phase (t = 15–19 s), use s = ut + ½at² to find the distance covered.
(d) Sketch the corresponding V-T graph, labelling all key values.

Challenge C2 [4 marks]

An A-T graph has equal positive and negative areas: a = +k ms⁻² for time T seconds, then a = −k ms⁻² for time T seconds.

(a) Show algebraically that the net change in velocity is zero regardless of k and T.
(b) If k = 3 and T = 5, and the object starts at v = 8 ms⁻¹, what is the velocity at the very midpoint (t = T = 5 s)?
(c) Describe what the corresponding V-T graph would look like.

✅ Answer Key — Homework

Part A — Calculation Problems

A1 ΔV₁ = +5 × 4 = +20 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₂ = −3 × 4 = −12 ms⁻¹.
Total ΔV = +20 − 12 = +8 ms⁻¹
A2 Start v = 0. ΔV₁ = +8×2 = +16 → v at 2s = 16 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₂ = 0 → v at 6s = 16 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₃ = −4×4 = −16 → v at 10s = 0 ms⁻¹.
A3 u = 12. ΔV₁ = −2×3 = −6 → v at 3s = 6 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₂ = +4×2 = +8 → v at 5s = 14 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₃ = 0 → v at 8s = 14 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₄ = −6×2 = −12 → v at 10s = 2 ms⁻¹.
A4 a₁ = (20−0)/5 = +4 ms⁻².
a₂ = 0/4 = 0 ms⁻².
a₃ = (−4−20)/6 = −24/6 = −4 ms⁻².
A5 Start v = 4 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₁ = +7×3 = +21 → v at 3s = 25 ms⁻¹.
ΔV₂ = −7×3 = −21 → v at 6s = 4 ms⁻¹.
Net ΔV = +21 − 21 = 0. The object returns to its original velocity.
A6 Start v = 2 ms⁻¹.
0→4s: ΔV = +12 → v = 14.   4→7s: ΔV = −12 → v = 2.   7→10s: ΔV = 0 → v = 2.   10→12s: ΔV = +10 → v = 12.
v at t = 12 s = 12 ms⁻¹.
V-T shape: rises steeply to 14 at t=4, falls back to 2 at t=7, flat 2→10s, rises again to 12 at t=12.

Part B — True or False

B1 FALSE. a = 0 means constant velocity (which could be any value, including non-zero). The object is stationary only if v = 0.
B2 TRUE. ΔV = a × t = −4 × 3 = −12 ms⁻¹. ✓
B3 FALSE. Zero slope on a V-T graph means a = 0, so the A-T graph shows a horizontal line ON the x-axis (a = 0), not at a non-zero value.
B4 TRUE. Total area = total ΔV. If ΔV = 0, then v_final = v_initial.

Part C — Challenge

C1 (a) ΔV₁ = +2×5 = +10. Starting at 0, max speed = 10 ms⁻¹ at t = 5s.
(b) Braking ΔV = −2.5×4 = −10. v_final = 10 − 10 = 0 ms⁻¹ (comes to rest).
(c) During braking: u = 10 ms⁻¹, a = −2.5 ms⁻², t = 4s.
    s = 10×4 + ½×(−2.5)×16 = 40 − 20 = 20 m.
(d) V-T: rises from 0 to 10 ms⁻¹ at t=5s (slope +2), flat at 10 ms⁻¹ from t=5–15s, falls to 0 at t=19s (slope −2.5).
C2 (a) ΔV_total = (+k×T) + (−k×T) = kT − kT = 0 for any k, T. ✓
(b) k = 3, T = 5. ΔV at t = 5s = +3×5 = +15. v = 8 + 15 = 23 ms⁻¹.
(c) V-T: starts at 8 ms⁻¹, rises linearly to peak of 23 ms⁻¹ at t=5s (slope +3), then falls linearly back to 8 ms⁻¹ at t=10s (slope −3). Symmetric inverted V shape.
