Translating Position & Velocity Graphs

Practice — Converting between P‑T and V‑T representations

 
 
 

Quick Reference

  • Slope of P‑T graph = velocity
  • Slope of V‑T graph = acceleration
  • Area under V‑T curve = displacement (Δp)
  • PT→VT: slope of each P‑T segment → horizontal line on V‑T graph
  • VT→PT: velocity = slope of P‑T segment  OR  accumulate Δp = v × Δt

Answer Key — Unit 8 Classwork

Part A

A1a: v = Δp/Δt = (8−0)/(4−0) = +2 ms⁻¹
A1b: V‑T graph shows a horizontal line at v = +2 ms⁻¹ from t = 0 to 4 s, then a horizontal line at v = 0 ms⁻¹ from t = 4 to 8 s.
A2: v₁ = 6/3 = +2 ms⁻¹ (above axis); v₂ = (0−6)/3 = −2 ms⁻¹ (below axis); v₃ = 0 ms⁻¹ (on axis)
A3: Using area method: Δp₁ = 4×3 = +12 m (p: 0→12 m); Δp₂ = 0×4 = 0 m (p stays 12 m); Δp₃ = −2×3 = −6 m (p: 12→6 m). P‑T: rises steeply to 12 m at t=3, flat to t=7, then falls to 6 m at t=10.
A4a: v₁ = 0 ms⁻¹ (flat); v₂ = (15−5)/2 = +5 ms⁻¹; v₃ = (5−15)/4 = −2.5 ms⁻¹
A5: p₀ = 4 m. Δp₁ = 2×5 = +10 → p(5s) = 14 m. Δp₂ = −3×4 = −12 → p(9s) = 2 m. Δp₃ = 1×3 = +3 → p(12s) = 5 m.
A6a: Δp₁ = 6×2 = +12 m; Δp₂ = 0×3 = 0 m; Δp₃ = −4×3 = −12 m. A6b: p(8s) = 0 + 12 + 0 − 12 = 0 m (back to start)

Part C

C1: The student is incorrect. A horizontal line on the V‑T graph means constant, non-zero velocity. On the P‑T graph this corresponds to a straight line with a non-zero slope (v = +3 ms⁻¹ → upward-sloping line). A horizontal P‑T line would mean v = 0.
C2: The V‑T graph shows a horizontal line at a large negative velocity value (below the time axis). The object is moving in the negative direction at constant speed — it is not accelerating. The steeper the P‑T slope, the further below the axis the V‑T line sits.
C3: Slope method: use the velocity as the slope (steepness) of each P‑T segment — easier when you want to draw the shape quickly. Area method: calculate the area of each V‑T block (v × Δt) to find displacement, then add to the running total — easier when you need exact position values or when sections are long. Both always give the same result.
