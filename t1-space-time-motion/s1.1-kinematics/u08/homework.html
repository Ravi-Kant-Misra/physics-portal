Translating Position & Velocity Graphs

Independent Practice — Due next class

 
 
 

Quick Reference

  • Slope of P‑T = velocity
  • Slope of V‑T = acceleration
  • Area under V‑T = displacement Δp
  • PT→VT: slope → horizontal line
  • VT→PT: velocity = slope  or  Δp = v·Δt

Homework Tip

Always show your working for slope and area calculations. Label each segment clearly on sketched graphs. Check: does the sign of your velocity match the direction of the P‑T slope?

Part A

A1a: v₁ = (10−0)/5 = +2 ms⁻¹; v₂ = (2−10)/4 = −2 ms⁻¹
A1b: V‑T shows a horizontal line at +2 ms⁻¹ for t = 0–5 s, then a horizontal line at −2 ms⁻¹ for t = 5–9 s. Jump (vertical dashed line) at t = 5 s.
A2: Δp₁ = −5×3 = −15 m → p(3s) = 20−15 = 5 m. Δp₂ = +5×4 = +20 m → p(7s) = 5+20 = 25 m.
A3: Δp₁ = 2×4 = 8 → p(4s) = 8 m. Δp₂ = 6×2 = 12 → p(6s) = 20 m. Δp₃ = 0×3 = 0 → p(9s) = 20 m. Δp₄ = −3×2 = −6 → p(11s) = 14 m.
A4: v₁ = 0 ms⁻¹ (flat at 3 m); v₂ = (9−3)/3 = +2 ms⁻¹; v₃ = (0−9)/2 = −4.5 ms⁻¹; v₄ = 0 ms⁻¹ (flat at 0 m).
A5: Δp₁ = 4×3 = 12 m → p(3s) = 12 m. Δp₂ = −2×4 = −8 m → p(7s) = 4 m. Δp₃ = 3×3 = 9 m → p(10s) = 13 m. P‑T: rises steeply to 12 m, drops to 4 m, then rises again to 13 m.
A6a: p_X = 3×8 = 24 m; p_Y = 5×3 + 0×5 = 15 m. A6b: Object X is ahead by 24−15 = 9 m.

Part B — True / False

B1: FALSE — A horizontal line at v = 0 ms⁻¹ means the object is at rest. A horizontal line at any other value means the object moves at constant, non-zero velocity — position IS changing (P‑T graph has a non-zero slope).
B2: TRUE — Speed is the magnitude of velocity. A steeper slope (positive OR negative) means a greater rate of change of position = greater speed.
B3: FALSE — The area under a V‑T graph gives the displacement (change in position, including sign/direction). To find total distance you must sum the magnitudes of each area separately (areas above and below the axis both counted as positive).
B4: TRUE — In the slope method, the velocity value from the V‑T graph becomes the slope (Δp/Δt) of the corresponding segment on the P‑T graph.

Part C — Challenge

C1a: Δp₁ = +3×4 = +12 m → p(4s) = 10+12 = 22 m. Δp₂ = −5×2 = −10 m → p(6s) = 22−10 = 12 m. Δp₃ = +2×4 = +8 m → p(10s) = 12+8 = 20 m. Δp₄ = 0 → p(12s) = 20 m.
C1b: The object never returns exactly to p = 10 m after t = 0 in this scenario — the lowest point is 12 m (at t = 6 s), which is still above 10 m. If the question is about crossing p = 10 m going downward during segment 2: segment 2 starts at 22 m and drops at −5 ms⁻¹. Time to drop from 22 to 10 m: Δp = −12 m → t = 12/5 = 2.4 s after t = 4 s → t = 6.4 s (but in this case it only lasts to t = 6 s, reaching 12 m). So the object does NOT return to 10 m.
C2a: v₁ = (15−0)/(5−0) = +3 ms⁻¹. v₂ = (3−15)/(9−5) = −12/4 = −3 ms⁻¹.
C2b: V‑T graph: horizontal line at +3 ms⁻¹ from t = 0–5 s; horizontal line at −3 ms⁻¹ from t = 5–9 s. (Equal speed, opposite directions.)
C2c: Total displacement = final p − initial p = 3 − 0 = +3 m. Via area: Δp = (+3)(5) + (−3)(4) = 15 − 12 = +3 m. ✓ Both methods agree.
C2d: Continuing at v = −3 ms⁻¹ from t = 9 to 12 s (3 s): Δp = −3×3 = −9 m → p(12s) = 3+(−9) = −6 m.
