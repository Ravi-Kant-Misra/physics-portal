Part A — Calculation Problems

Problem 1 — v = u + at
v = 20 ms⁻¹
u=0, a=2, t=10. v = 0 + 2×10 = 20 ms⁻¹. Geometrically: the line rises from 0 by a×t = 2×10 = 20.
Problem 2 — v = u + at (deceleration)
v = 2 ms⁻¹
u=8, a=−1.5, t=4. v = 8 + (−1.5)×4 = 8 − 6 = 2 ms⁻¹. The line has negative slope; it falls by 6 over 4 s.
Problem 3 — Rectangle + Triangle
s = 57.5 m
Rectangle = u×t = 4×5 = 20 m. Triangle = ½×a×t² = ½×3×25 = 37.5 m. Total = 20 + 37.5 = 57.5 m.
Problem 4 — From rest: rectangle = 0
s = 90 m
u=0 → rectangle = 0×6 = 0. Triangle = ½×5×36 = 90 m. s = 0 + 90 = 90 m.
Problem 5 — Trapezoid
s = 24 m
s = (u+v)/2 × t = (2+10)/2 × 4 = 6 × 4 = 24 m.
Problem 6 — Trapezoid
s = 150 m
s = (u+v)/2 × t = (5+25)/2 × 10 = 15 × 10 = 150 m.

Part B — Graph Problems

B1 — Reading v-t graph (u=3, a=2, t=5)
(a) v=13 ms⁻¹; (b) 15 m; (c) 25 m; (d) 40 m
(a) v=3+2×5=13. (b) rectangle=3×5=15. (c) triangle=½×2×25=25. (d) s=15+25=40 m. Check: s=(3+13)/2×5=8×5=40 ✓
B2 — Trapezoid (u=6, v=18, t=8)
(a) 6 & 18; (b) t=8 s; (c) s=96 m
s=(6+18)/2×8=12×8=96 m. The parallel sides are the two velocity values; height is time.
B3 — Triangle only (u=0, v=20, t=4)
(a) 4 s; (b) 20 ms⁻¹; (c) 40 m; (d) 5 ms⁻²
s=½×4×20=40 m. a=v/t=20/4=5 ms⁻². When u=0, the rectangle vanishes and only the triangle remains.
B4 — Line from (0,10) to (6,22)
(a) 10; (b) 22; (c) 12; (d) 2 ms⁻²; (e) 96 m
Rise=22−10=12. Run=6. a=12/6=2 ms⁻². s=(10+22)/2×6=16×6=96 m.

Part C — Short Answer Model Answers

C1. The area under a v-t graph equals displacement because displacement is the product of velocity and time (s = v×t for constant velocity). When velocity changes linearly (constant acceleration), we split the area into geometric shapes and sum them. The rectangle gives ut (the displacement if there were no acceleration) and the triangle gives ½at² (the extra displacement due to acceleration). Together: s = ut + ½at².
C2. For non-zero initial velocity u and positive acceleration a: (1) A blue rectangle sits below the horizontal dashed line at height u. Its area = base × height = t × u = ut, representing the displacement the object would have if it moved at constant initial velocity the whole time. (2) A green triangle sits above the dashed line. Its height = at (the rise from the slope proof) and base = t. Area = ½×t×at = ½at², the additional displacement from accelerating.
C3. From Equation 1, rearrange to get t = (v−u)/a. Substitute this into Equation 3 (s = (u+v)/2 × t): s = (u+v)/2 × (v−u)/a. Multiply both sides by 2a: 2as = (v+u)(v−u) = v²−u². Rearranging gives v² = u² + 2as.
