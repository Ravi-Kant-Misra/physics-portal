Problem 1 — v = u + at; Rise = at
v = 33 ms⁻¹
Rise = a×t = 3.5×6 = 21. v = u + Rise = 12 + 21 = 33 ms⁻¹. Geometrically: the v-t line climbs 21 units vertically over 6 s.
Problem 2 — Rectangle + Triangle
s = 96 m
Rectangle = 6×8 = 48 m. Height of triangle = at = 1.5×8 = 12. Triangle = ½×8×12 = 48 m. s = 48+48 = 96 m.
Problem 3 — Trapezoid (plane)
s = 1500 m
s = (40+80)/2 × 25 = 60 × 25 = 1500 m. Parallel sides = u=40 (shorter) and v=80 (longer). Height = t=25.
Problem 4 — Slope of v-t graph
a = 3 ms⁻²
Rise = v₂−v₁ = 35−5 = 30. Run = t₂−t₁ = 10−0 = 10. a = 30/10 = 3 ms⁻². This is the slope of the line segment.
Problem 5 — Rectangle only (a = 0)
s = 180 m
Rectangle = 15×12 = 180 m. Triangle = ½×0×144 = 0 m. When a=0, the v-t line is horizontal so no triangle forms above it. The height "at" = 0.

Part B — True / False

B1. "The slope of a v-t graph represents displacement."
FALSE
Corrected: The slope of a velocity-time graph represents acceleration. Displacement is the area under the v-t graph, not the slope.
B2. "Blue rectangle area = ut = displacement due to initial velocity."
TRUE
Correct. The rectangle (base = t, height = u) has area = u×t = ut. This is the displacement the object would have if there were no acceleration at all — i.e. due to the initial velocity alone.
B3. "In the trapezoid proof, the height of the trapezoid = acceleration a."
FALSE
Corrected: The height of the trapezoid is the time interval t (the horizontal distance between the two parallel velocity sides). The two parallel sides are u and v; the height is t.
B4. "v² = u² + 2as is derived by substituting t from v=u+at into the trapezoid equation."
TRUE
Correct. From Eq.1: t = (v−u)/a. Substituting into Eq.3: s = (u+v)/2 × (v−u)/a. So 2as = (v+u)(v−u) = v²−u². Rearranging: v² = u² + 2as.

Part C — Challenge Problems

Challenge 1 — Rocket from rest to 60 ms⁻¹ in 12 s
(a) a = v/t = 60/12 = 5 ms⁻² (slope: rise = 60−0 = 60, run = 12, a = 60/12)
(b) u=0 → rectangle = 0. Triangle area = ½×t×v = ½×12×60 = 360 m. (Or: ½at² = ½×5×144 = 360 m)
(c) s = (0+60)/2 × 12 = 30 × 12 = 360 m ✓ (both methods agree)
(d) Rectangle area = 0 (since u=0). Triangle = 360 m = 100% of total area. When u=0 the trapezoid degenerates to a pure triangle.
a = 5 ms⁻² | s = 360 m | Triangle = 100% of area
Challenge 2 — Car A vs Car B over 5 s

(b) Car A: u=0 → rectangle = 0. Triangle = ½×5×30 = 75 m. (Or: ½at² = ½×6×25 = 75 m)
(c) Car B: a=0 → rectangle = 20×5 = 100 m. Triangle = 0 (horizontal line, no rise).
(d) Car B: 100 m. Car A: 75 m. Car B is ahead by 100−75 = 25 m after 5 s. On the v-t graph, Car B's rectangle (100 m) is larger than Car A's triangle (75 m).
