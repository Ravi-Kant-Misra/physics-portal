A-1

C slope = Δp/Δt = (10 − 2) / (4 − 0) = 8/4 = +2 m/s. The positive slope of the p–t line is the constant velocity.

A-2

C A concave-down p–t curve means the slope is positive but decreasing. The velocity starts positive and decreases toward zero — this is a negatively sloped line on the v–t graph starting above zero and falling.

A-3

Segment 1 (0–2 s): Positive constant slope on p–t → line going up with slope = +6 m/s. (Δp = 6 × 2 = 12 m)
Segment 2 (2–4 s): Zero slope on p–t → flat horizontal line (no displacement change).
Segment 3 (4–6 s): Negative constant slope on p–t → line going down with slope = −4 m/s. (Δp = −4 × 2 = −8 m)

A-4

B a = Δv/Δt = (0 − 20) / (5 − 0) = −20/5 = −4 m/s². The car decelerates (negative acceleration).

A-5

C Area under a–t = ΔV = 3 m/s² × 5 s = 15 m/s. Starting from rest: v_f = 0 + 15 = 15 m/s.

A-6

C Area under v–t = displacement. The v–t graph forms a triangle with base = 8 s, height = 8 m/s.
Area = ½ × 8 × 8 = 32 m.

B-1

Segment 1: slope = (12 − 0)/(3 − 0) = +4 m/s → horizontal line at v = +4 m/s from t = 0 to 3 s.
Segment 2: slope = 0/3 = 0 m/s → horizontal line at v = 0 from t = 3 to 6 s.
Segment 3: slope = (6 − 12)/(9 − 6) = −6/3 = −2 m/s → horizontal line at v = −2 m/s from t = 6 to 9 s.
The v–t graph is a step function: +4 → 0 → −2 m/s.

B-2

Constant v = −3 m/s → straight negative slope on p–t.
p₀ = 15 m. Δp = v × t = −3 × 6 = −18 m.
p_f = 15 + (−18) = −3 m.
The p–t graph is a straight line from (0, 15) to (6, −3).

B-3

a) Slope = Δv/Δt = (−6 − 12)/(6 − 0) = −18/6 = −3 m/s².
b) The a–t graph is a horizontal line at a = −3 m/s² from t = 0 to 6 s (constant negative acceleration).

B-4

Phase 1 (0 → 3 s): ΔV = area = +4 × 3 = +12 m/s. v at t=3: 2 + 12 = 14 m/s.
Phase 2 (3 → 6 s): ΔV = area = −2 × 3 = −6 m/s. v at t=6: 14 + (−6) = 8 m/s.
The v–t graph: line rising from 2 to 14 (t=0 to 3), then line falling from 14 to 8 (t=3 to 6). Both segments are straight (constant acceleration each phase).

C-1

A curved p–t graph means the slope (velocity) is changing. On the v–t graph, a changing velocity appears as a slanted line (non-horizontal), because the velocity value itself is not constant — it is increasing or decreasing over time. If the velocity were constant, the p–t graph would be a straight (non-curved) line.

C-2

a) Moving in the negative direction (v is negative).
b) Acceleration = 0 (horizontal v–t line has zero slope).
c) The p–t graph is a straight line with a negative slope (position decreasing at a constant rate).

C-3

a) Area under a–t = ΔV = 6 m/s² × 4 s = 24 m/s. Starting from rest: v at end of phase 1 = 24 m/s.
b) Displacement during acceleration phase (triangle on v–t): ½ × 4 × 24 = 48 m.
Displacement during constant phase (rectangle): 24 m/s × 3 s = 72 m.
Total displacement = 48 + 72 = 120 m.
