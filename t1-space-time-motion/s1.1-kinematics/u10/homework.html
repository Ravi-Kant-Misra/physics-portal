Answer Key — Click to Reveal ▼

A-1

B A horizontal p–t line means zero slope → zero velocity. The v–t graph is a horizontal line at v = 0 m/s. The 8 m is the position, not the velocity.

A-2

a) a = Δv/Δt = (16 − 0) / (8 − 0) = +2 m/s².
b) The a–t graph is a horizontal line at a = +2 m/s² from t = 0 to 8 s (constant positive acceleration).
c) Displacement = area under v–t = triangle area = ½ × 8 × 16 = 64 m.

A-3

Phase 1 (0–4 s): ΔV = area = −5 × 4 = −20 m/s. v at t=4: 20 + (−20) = 0 m/s.
Phase 2 (4–8 s): a = 0, so ΔV = 0. v stays at 0 m/s.
The v–t graph: straight line falling from +20 to 0 (t=0 to 4), then horizontal at 0 (t=4 to 8).

A-4

B A concave-up p–t curve means the slope (velocity) is positive and increasing. This gives a v–t graph that is a rising line (positive slope), meaning constant positive acceleration on the a–t graph.

A-5

Phase 1 (0–2 s): constant v → a = 0 m/s².
Phase 2 (2–5 s): a = Δv/Δt = (−4 − 8) / (5 − 2) = −12/3 = −4 m/s².
Phase 3 (5–8 s): constant v → a = 0 m/s².
The a–t graph: horizontal at 0 (0–2 s), horizontal block at −4 m/s² (2–5 s), horizontal at 0 (5–8 s).

A-6

a) Phase 1 (0–3 s): ΔV = +6 × 3 = +18 m/s. v at t=3: 0 + 18 = 18 m/s (rising line on v–t).
Phase 2 (3–6 s): ΔV = −6 × 3 = −18 m/s. v at t=6: 18 − 18 = 0 m/s (falling line on v–t).
b) Maximum velocity = 18 m/s at t = 3 s (peak of tent-shaped v–t graph).
c) Velocity at t = 6 s = 0 m/s.

B-1

TRUE The area under the v–t graph equals displacement (Δp). Areas above the axis are positive displacement; areas below are negative. The total (signed) area = Δp.

B-2

FALSE A straight line on a p–t graph means constant velocity — which could be zero (horizontal) or any nonzero constant (positive or negative slope). Only a horizontal straight line means v = 0.

B-3

TRUE A horizontal v–t line has slope = 0, and slope of v–t = acceleration. So a = 0, which appears as a horizontal line at zero on the a–t graph.

B-4

FALSE A parabola (curved) on the p–t graph means the slope (velocity) is changing, which requires nonzero acceleration. Zero acceleration would produce a straight line, not a parabola.

C-1

a) Phase 1 (0–5 s): ΔV = +2 × 5 = +10 m/s. v(5) = 0 + 10 = +10 m/s. V–t is a rising line from 0 to 10 m/s.
Phase 2 (5–10 s): a = 0, ΔV = 0. v stays at +10 m/s. V–t is a horizontal line at 10 m/s.

b) Phase 1: a ≠ 0 → p–t is a parabola (concave up) — slope increases from 0.
Phase 2: a = 0 (constant v) → p–t is a straight line with positive slope.

c) Area under v–t: Triangle (0–5 s): ½ × 5 × 10 = 25 m. Rectangle (5–10 s): 10 × 5 = 50 m. Total displacement = 25 + 50 = 75 m.

d) At t = 7 s, the a–t graph shows a = 0 m/s² (in the second phase). The object is not accelerating at t = 7 s — it moves at constant velocity.

C-2

Phase 1 (acceleration, 0–4 s): v = +3 × 4 = +12 m/s at end.
Phase 2 (constant, 4–10 s): v = +12 m/s for 6 s.
Phase 3 (deceleration): starts at v = +12 m/s, a = −1.5 m/s².

a) Time to stop: t = Δv/a = (0 − 12)/(−1.5) = 8 s for the deceleration phase.

b) A–t graph: Phase 1: horizontal block at +3 m/s². Phase 2: horizontal line at 0 m/s². Phase 3: horizontal block at −1.5 m/s².

c) V–t graph: Phase 1: straight line rising from 0 to 12 m/s (t=0–4 s). Phase 2: horizontal line at 12 m/s (t=4–10 s). Phase 3: straight line falling from 12 to 0 m/s (t=10–18 s).

d) P–t graph: Phase 1: concave up (parabola — velocity increasing from 0). Phase 2: straight line with positive slope (constant velocity). Phase 3: concave down (parabola — velocity decreasing to 0).

e) Total distance: Phase 1: ½ × 4 × 12 = 24 m (triangle on v–t). Phase 2: 12 × 6 = 72 m (rectangle). Phase 3: ½ × 8 × 12 = 48 m (triangle). Total = 24 + 72 + 48 = 144 m.
