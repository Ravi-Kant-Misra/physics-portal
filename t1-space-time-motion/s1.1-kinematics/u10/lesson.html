Translating Position, Velocity & Acceleration Graphs

Reading and converting between all three motion graphs

📋

Core Summary Rules

Position vs Time
Slope = Velocity
The steepness (slope) of the p–t graph at any point tells you the instantaneous velocity at that moment. Steeper = faster.
Velocity vs Time
Slope = Acceleration
Area = ΔPosition
The slope of the v–t graph gives acceleration. The area between the curve and the time axis equals the displacement.
Acceleration vs Time
Area = ΔVelocity
The area between the a–t graph and the time axis equals the change in velocity over that time interval.
📐

Slope Reference Guide

When translating graphs, first identify the slope of each segment. The slope becomes the value on the next graph down.

Slope Type Visual Resulting Value
Steep positive Large positive (+)
Gentle positive Small positive (+)
Horizontal (flat) Zero (0)
Gentle negative Small negative (−)
Steep negative Large negative (−)
