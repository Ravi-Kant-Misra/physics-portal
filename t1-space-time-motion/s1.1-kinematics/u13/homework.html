TF 7 — "Not in free fall while moving upward"
FALSE
Correct statement: A ball thrown upward IS in free fall while moving upward, because only gravity acts on it after release. Free fall is defined by the FORCE acting (gravity only), not the direction of velocity. Having v and a in opposite directions just means the ball is decelerating — it is still in free fall.
TF 8 — "At the top, both v and a are zero"
FALSE
Correct statement: At the highest point, v = 0 m/s (instantaneously), BUT the acceleration is still 9.81 m/s² downward. Gravity never stops acting during free fall. Acceleration is zero only if there are no net forces — but gravity is still present.
TF 9 — "Velocity increases by 9.81 m/s every second"
TRUE
This is TRUE. The acceleration due to gravity is 9.81 m/s². By definition, acceleration = change in velocity per second. So every second, the speed increases by 9.81 m/s. After 1 s: v = 9.81 m/s; after 2 s: v = 19.62 m/s; etc.
TF 10 — "Heavier ball falls faster"
FALSE
Correct statement: In free fall (neglecting air resistance), all objects fall with the same acceleration g = 9.81 m/s², regardless of mass. Both balls hit the ground at the same time. Galileo demonstrated this. While gravity does pull harder on heavier objects, heavier objects also have more inertia — the effects cancel exactly (a = F/m = mg/m = g).
Challenge 11 — Return to same height (u=16 m/s)
(a) t = 3.26 s | (b) v = −16 m/s (16 m/s downward)
(a) s = ut + ½at²; at return s = 0: 0 = 16t + ½(−9.81)t² 0 = 16t − 4.905t² 0 = t(16 − 4.905t) t = 0 (at launch) OR t = 16/4.905 = 3.26 s (b) v = u + at = 16 + (−9.81)(3.26) = 16 − 31.98 = −16.0 m/s Speed = 16 m/s, direction = downward. (This confirms free-fall symmetry: same speed at same height.)
Challenge 12 — Two balls, Ball B thrown down at 5 m/s
Ball B hits first. Difference ≈ 0.56 s
Ball A (u=0): 80 = ½(9.81)t² t² = 80/4.905 = 16.31 t_A = 4.04 s Ball B (u=5 m/s down): 80 = 5t + ½(9.81)t² 80 = 5t + 4.905t² 4.905t² + 5t − 80 = 0 t = [−5 ± √(25 + 4×4.905×80)] / (2×4.905) t = [−5 ± √(25 + 1569.6)] / 9.81 t = [−5 ± √1594.6] / 9.81 t = [−5 ± 39.93] / 9.81 t = 34.93/9.81 = 3.56 s (taking positive root) Difference = 4.04 − 3.56 = 0.48 s (Ball B hits the ground approximately 0.48 s before Ball A.)
