Answer Key — Free Fall Classwork

Q1 — Dropped stone, 45 m
v = 29.7 m/s downward
v² = u² + 2as = 0 + 2(9.81)(45) = 882.9 v = √882.9 = 29.7 m/s
Q2 — Ball reaches 25 m/s, find distance
s = 31.8 m
v² = u² + 2as 25² = 0 + 2(9.81)s 625 = 19.62s s = 625 / 19.62 = 31.8 m
Q3 — Thrown up at 18 m/s, max height
s = 16.5 m
v² = u² + 2as (up = +, v=0 at peak) 0 = 18² + 2(−9.81)s 0 = 324 − 19.62s 19.62s = 324 s = 324 / 19.62 = 16.5 m
Q4 — Thrown up at 12 m/s, time to peak
t = 1.22 s
v = u + at 0 = 12 + (−9.81)t 9.81t = 12 t = 12 / 9.81 = 1.22 s
Q5 — Dropped, t = 3.5 s, find height
s = 60.1 m
s = ut + ½at² s = 0 + ½(9.81)(3.5²) s = 4.905 × 12.25 = 60.1 m
Q6 — Cliff problem: u=8 m/s up, cliff=30 m, total time
Total time = 3.06 s
Part 1 (up to peak): 0 = 8 + (−9.81)t₁ → t₁ = 8/9.81 = 0.816 s Height above cliff: 0²=8²+2(−9.81)s → s=64/19.62=3.26 m above cliff Part 2 (peak to ground): s = −(3.26+30) = −33.26 m, u=0 −33.26 = ½(−9.81)t₂² t₂² = 33.26/4.905 = 6.78 t₂ = 2.60 s (but using s=ut+½at²: t₂=√(2×33.26/9.81)=2.60 s) Total = 0.816 + 2.60 ≈ 3.42 s Note: Using s = ut+½at² directly for full trip (u=+8, a=−9.81, s=−30): −30 = 8t − 4.905t² 4.905t² − 8t − 30 = 0 t = [8 ± √(64 + 4×4.905×30)] / (2×4.905) t = [8 ± √(64+588.6)] / 9.81 t = [8 ± √652.6] / 9.81 t = [8 ± 25.55] / 9.81 t = 33.55/9.81 = 3.42 s
Q7 — VT graph analysis
(a) The slope = acceleration. (b) Slope = Δv/Δt = (−20−20)/(4.08−0) = −9.81 m/s². (c) v=0 at t ≈ 2.04 s (where line crosses t-axis).
slope = rise/run = (−20−20)/(4.08−0) = −40/4.08 = −9.8 m/s² This equals the acceleration due to gravity (negative because up=+).
Q8 — Position intervals for dropped ball
A = 4.9 m, B = 14.7 m, C = 24.5 m
Distance in each second interval using s=ut+½at²: 0→1 s: s=½(9.81)(1)²=4.905≈4.9 m 1→2 s: s(0→2)=½(9.81)(4)=19.62m; interval = 19.62−4.905=14.7 m 2→3 s: s(0→3)=½(9.81)(9)=44.1m; interval = 44.1−19.62=24.5 m The gaps increase (1:3:5 ratio) — characteristic of uniform acceleration.
Q9 — AT graph description
The AT graph is a horizontal line at a = −9.81 m/s² throughout (0 to 3 s). Constant downward acceleration throughout free fall.
Free fall = constant acceleration. The AT graph is always a horizontal line at −9.81 m/s² (with up=+), regardless of whether the ball moves up or down.

Q11 — Conceptual: upward throw still in free fall
While moving upward, only gravity acts — no thrust or contact force from the thrower remains after release. Gravity (9.81 m/s² downward) acts continuously, decelerating the upward-moving ball. Velocity decreases because the acceleration (downward) is opposite to the direction of motion (upward).
Q12 — Misconception: zero acceleration at top
The student is INCORRECT. Velocity = 0 at the top, but acceleration ≠ 0. Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity. Gravity continuously acts, so acceleration = 9.81 m/s² downward even when v = 0. The instant of zero velocity does not mean forces have stopped acting.
Q13 — Symmetry of free fall trajectory
Time up = time down (symmetric trajectory). Speed at launch = speed at landing (same magnitude). This is because with constant acceleration and no air resistance, the motion is perfectly reversible. The ball decelerates uniformly going up and accelerates uniformly coming down — the physics is the same in both directions.
Proof: if thrown at u=v₀, time to peak t=v₀/g. Falling from rest through same height h=v₀²/2g gives v=√(2gh)=v₀. ✓
