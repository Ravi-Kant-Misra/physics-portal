Kinematics · Unit 13

Free Fall

Gravity, acceleration, and motion under a single force

What is Free Fall?
Free Fall: If the only force acting on an object is the force of gravity, the object is in free fall.

A book sitting on a table is not in free fall — the table pushes back with a contact (normal) force. A thrown ball after leaving the hand is in free fall — only gravity acts on it (ignoring air resistance).

The Acceleration of Gravity
Symbol
g
Value on Earth
9.81 m/s²
Direction
Always DOWN
⚠

The acceleration due to gravity is constant throughout free fall — even when the object is moving upward, decelerating, or momentarily at rest at its peak.

Three Free Fall Rules

Knowing that an object is in free fall gives you information you can plug straight into the kinematic equations:

  • 1 The acceleration is always g = 9.81 m/s², and always points downward.
  • 2 If the object is dropped (released from rest), its initial velocity u = 0 m/s.
  • 3 If the object is thrown upward, its velocity at the highest point is v = 0 m/s (instantaneously at rest before falling back down).
Sign Conventions

You can choose either direction as positive, but you must be consistent within a problem.

Down = +

Use when object is dropped or motion is primarily downward. Then a = +9.81 m/s².

Up = +

Use when object is thrown upward. Then a = −9.81 m/s² (acceleration opposes motion).

Kinematic Formulas Used
FormulaVariablesUse when you need to find
v² = u² + 2as v, u, a, s Final velocity or displacement (no time)
v = u + at v, u, a, t Final velocity or time
s = ut + ½at² s, u, a, t Displacement when time is known
Worked Example 1 — Dropped Ball

Example 1

A ball is dropped from a height of 10 m. What will its velocity be right before it hits the ground?
Sign convention: Down = +
a = 9.81 m/s²
s = 10 m
u = 0 m/s (dropped)
v = ?

Formula: v² = u² + 2as

v² = 0² + 2(9.81)(10) v² = 196.2 v = √196.2 v = 14 m/s
v = 14 m/s downward
Worked Example 2 — Maximum Height

Example 2

A ball is thrown up at 10 m/s. What is the maximum height it will reach?
Sign convention: Up = +
u = +10 m/s
a = −9.81 m/s²
v = 0 m/s (at peak)
s = ?

Formula: v² = u² + 2as

0² = 10² + 2(−9.81)s 0 = 100 − 19.62s 19.62s = 100 s = 100 / 19.62 s = 5.1 m
Maximum height = 5.1 m
Worked Example 3 — Time to Maximum Height

Example 3

A ball is thrown up at 10 m/s. How much time will it take to reach its maximum height?
Sign convention: Up = +
u = +10 m/s
a = −9.8 m/s²
v = 0 m/s
t = ?

Formula: v = u + at

0 = 10 + (−9.8)t 9.8t = 10 t = 10 / 9.8 t = 1.02 s
t = 1.02 seconds
Worked Example 4 — Ball Thrown Off a Cliff (Multi-Part)

Example 4 — Cliff Problem

A ball is thrown upward off a cliff at 20 m/s. The cliff is 50 m high. How much time does it take the ball to reach the ground at the bottom of the cliff?
Sign convention: Up = +

--- Part 1: Time to reach maximum height ---

u = +20 m/s
a = −9.81 m/s²
v = 0 m/s
t = ?

Formula: v = u + at

0 = 20 + (−9.81)t 9.81t = 20 t = 20 / 9.81 t₁ = 2.04 s

--- Part 2: Time to fall from peak to ground ---

First find the height reached above the cliff (using v² = u² + 2as with Part 1 data):
0² = 20² + 2(−9.81)s → s = 400/19.62 = 20.4 m above cliff top

Total displacement from peak to ground = −(20.4 + 50) = −70.4 m

u = 0 m/s (starts from rest at peak)
a = −9.81 m/s²
s = −70.4 m
t = ?

Formula: s = ut + ½at²

−70.4 = 0·t + ½(−9.81)t² −70.4 = −4.905t² t² = 70.4 / 4.905 t² = 14.35 t₂ = 3.78 s

--- Total Time ---

Total time = t₁ + t₂ = 2.04 + 3.78 = 5.82 s
Total time = 5.82 seconds
Key Conceptual Rules — Summary
✓

Free fall requires only gravity acting. Any contact force (hand, surface, air resistance) means the object is NOT in free fall.

✓

The acceleration g = 9.81 m/s² downward is constant throughout free fall — including during upward motion.

✓

At the highest point of any free-fall trajectory, the instantaneous velocity is 0 m/s, but the acceleration is still 9.81 m/s² downward.

💡

For multi-part problems (e.g., ball thrown from a cliff): split at the highest point where v = 0, solve each segment separately, then add the times.
