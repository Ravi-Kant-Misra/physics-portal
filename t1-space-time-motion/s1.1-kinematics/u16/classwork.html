Answer Key — Classwork Unit 16: Motion Maps

A1 — Constant Velocity
Speed = 3 m/s  |  Motion type: Constant velocity
Arrow length = 3 cm = 3 m displacement per 1 second interval. Therefore v = 3 m / 1 s = 3 m/s. All arrows are equal length, confirming constant (uniform) velocity. No acceleration is present.
A2 — Speeding Up
Yes, accelerating  |  Acceleration is positive
Displacements per second: t=0→1: 2m, t=1→2: 4m, t=2→3: 6m. Dot spacing is increasing, meaning the object covers more distance each second. This is positive acceleration (speeding up in the positive direction). Red arrows would point to the right.
A3 — At Rest
v = 0 m/s
No velocity arrows are drawn because displacement = 0 m each second. v = 0/1 = 0 m/s. All dots are at the same position x = 5 m for all four time steps. The object is completely stationary.
A4 — Slowing Down
Displacements: 8 m, 6 m, 4 m, 2 m  |  Acceleration: negative
t=0→1: 8-0=8m; t=1→2: 14-8=6m; t=2→3: 18-14=4m; t=3→4: 20-18=2m. Dot spacing decreases each second — the object is slowing down. Change in velocity per second = -2 m/s per second. Acceleration is negative (opposite to motion direction). Red arrows would point to the LEFT.
A5 — Arrow Direction
Slowing down
Red acceleration arrows pointing LEFT (negative direction) while velocity arrows point RIGHT (positive direction) means acceleration is opposite to velocity. This causes the velocity to decrease each second — the object is decelerating. Acceleration direction determines whether velocity grows or shrinks.
A6 — Velocity from Arrow Length
v = 7 m/s
Arrow length = 7 m (displacement per second). v = displacement / time = 7 m / 1 s = 7 m/s. On a motion map, the time interval between dots is always 1 second, so arrow length (= displacement) numerically equals the velocity in m/s. No division is needed because the denominator is always 1.
B7 — Draw: Constant Velocity 4 m/s
Dot positions: 0 m, 4 m, 8 m, 12 m, 16 m
At v = 4 m/s constant: position = v x t. t=0: 0m; t=1: 4m; t=2: 8m; t=3: 12m; t=4: 16m. All five velocity arrows should be the same length (4 units each). Equal spacing confirms constant velocity. No red acceleration arrows needed.
B8 — Draw: Speeding Up from Rest
Dot positions: 0 m, 1 m, 4 m, 9 m
Using kinematics with a = 2 m/s²: x = ½at². t=0: 0m; t=1: ½(2)(1)=1m; t=2: ½(2)(4)=4m; t=3: ½(2)(9)=9m. Alternatively, displacement each interval = avg v × 1s: 1m, 3m, 5m (cumulative: 0, 1, 4, 9). Velocity arrows get longer (2, 4, 6 units). Red arrows point RIGHT.
B9 — Draw: Slowing Down
Dot positions: 0 m, 5 m, 8 m, 9 m
Displacement each interval = avg v × 1s. 0→1: (6+4)/2=5m; 1→2: (4+2)/2=3m; 2→3: (2+0)/2=1m. Positions: 0, 5, 8, 9 m. Dot spacing decreases (5, 3, 1 m per second). Velocity arrows shrink in length (proportional to 6, 4, 2 m/s). Red acceleration arrows point LEFT (negative direction, opposing motion).
B10 — Identify Motion Type
Map X: Constant velocity, v = 5 m/s  |  Map Y: Speeding up, positive acceleration
Map X: Spacings are all 5 m per second (5, 5, 5, 5 m). Equal spacing = constant velocity. v = 5 m/1s = 5 m/s. Map Y: Spacings are 1, 2, 3, 4 m per second (increasing). Increasing spacing = speeding up. Acceleration = positive (arrows point rightward in direction of motion).
C11 — Velocity from Map (Short Answer)
Arrow length = speed because time = 1 second per interval
Expected answer: v = displacement / time. On a motion map, each time interval = 1 second. So v = displacement / 1 s = displacement (numerically). The arrow connects two dot positions 1 second apart, so its length equals the displacement covered in 1 second, which equals the velocity magnitude. This is why we can "read off" speed directly from arrow length.
C12 — Comparing Two Objects
Object B is faster  |  4 times faster  |  Arrow ratio A:B = 1:4
(a) Object B: 8 m/s vs 2 m/s — B is faster. (b) 8/2 = 4 times faster. (c) Arrow lengths are proportional to speed: A has arrows of length 2 units, B has length 8 units. Ratio A:B = 2:8 = 1:4.
C13 — Acceleration Vectors
(a) Slowing down  |  (b) -2 m/s per second  |  (c) Opposite to direction of motion (pointing left)
(a) Arrow lengths decrease: 10, 8, 6, 4 cm = 10, 8, 6, 4 m/s — velocity is decreasing each second — slowing down. (b) Change per second: 8-10 = -2 m/s per second. The acceleration = -2 m/s². (c) Since acceleration is negative (opposing the rightward motion), red acceleration arrows point to the LEFT — opposite to the direction of motion.
