Reference Formulas

v̲ = Δx / Δt
v̲(speed) = total distance / Δt
slope = Δy / Δx = rise / run
Inst. v = slope of tangent line
Avg. v = slope of secant line
Speed = |velocity| (≥ 0 always)
Study Tip: Remember — average uses information from TWO points (or an interval); instantaneous uses information at exactly ONE point. Speedometers, radar guns, and GPS all measure instantaneous speed!

Answer Key — Homework

A1 — Train average speed v̲ = d / t = 360 km / 4.5 h = 80 km/h
A2 — Average velocity from (1 s, 5 m) to (9 s, 29 m) v̲ = Δx / Δt = (29 − 5) / (9 − 1) = 24 / 8 = 3 m/s
A3 — Instantaneous velocity at t = 5 s

Slope of tangent through (3 s, 4 m) and (7 s, 12 m):

vᵢₙₛₜ = (12 − 4) / (7 − 3) = 8 / 4 = +2 m/s
A4 — Instantaneous velocity and speed at t = 7 s

Slope of tangent through (5 s, 10 m) and (9 s, 2 m):

vᵢₙₛₜ = (2 − 10) / (9 − 5) = −8 / 4 = −2 m/s

Instantaneous speed = |−2| = 2 m/s

A5 — Hiker multi-segment journey

(a) Total distance = 12 km + 0 km + 6 km = 18 km

(b) Final displacement = +12 km − 6 km = +6 km from start

(c) Total time = 2 + 1 + 1.5 = 4.5 h

Avg. speed = 18 km / 4.5 h = 4 km/h Avg. velocity = +6 km / 4.5 h ≈ +1.33 km/h
A6 — Average velocity and instantaneous velocity

(a) Slope of secant from (0 s, 2 m) to (6 s, 8 m):

v̲ = (8 − 2) / (6 − 0) = 6 / 6 = 1 m/s

(b) Slope of tangent through (1 s, 2 m) and (5 s, 8 m) at t = 3 s:

vᵢₙₛₜ = (8 − 2) / (5 − 1) = 6 / 4 = 1.5 m/s
B1 — TRUE or FALSE?

FALSE. Instantaneous speed is always non-negative. It is the magnitude (absolute value) of instantaneous velocity. Speed cannot be negative regardless of direction of motion.

B2 — TRUE or FALSE?

TRUE. The average velocity between two points on a P–T graph is found by drawing a secant line between those two points and calculating its slope.

B3 — TRUE or FALSE?

FALSE. At the peak (maximum) of a P–T curve, the tangent line is horizontal (slope = 0), so the instantaneous velocity is zero, not at its greatest positive value.

B4 — TRUE or FALSE?

TRUE. A speedometer shows the car's speed at that exact moment in time — this is instantaneous speed.

C1 — Challenge: full journey analysis

(a) Average velocity (t = 0 to t = 9 s):

v̲ = (1 − 0) / (9 − 0) = 1 / 9 ≈ 0.11 m/s

(b) t = 4 s is in the flat section (t = 3 to 5 s) where position is constant at 9 m. The tangent is horizontal → slope = 0. Instantaneous velocity = 0 m/s.

(c) At t = 7 s: tangent slope is given as −2 m/s → instantaneous velocity = −2 m/s.

(d) Total distance = 9 m (rise) + 8 m (fall) = 17 m; total time = 9 s.

Avg. speed = 17 / 9 ≈ 1.89 m/s
C2 — Challenge: tangent slope analysis

(a) Compare speeds: |+4| = 4 m/s, |0| = 0 m/s, |−3| = 3 m/s → fastest at t = 2 s with speed 4 m/s.

(b) At t = 5 s the slope is 0, so the object is momentarily at rest.

(c) Average velocity from (3 s, 6 m) to (7 s, 10 m):

v̲ = (10 − 6) / (7 − 3) = 4 / 4 = 1 m/s

(d) Velocity = −3 m/s → object moving in the negative direction. Instantaneous speed = |−3| = 3 m/s.
