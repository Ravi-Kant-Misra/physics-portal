Classwork — Average vs. Instantaneous Velocity

Name: _________________________    Date: _____________    Period: _______

Reference Formulas

v̲ = Δx / Δt
v̲ = total distance / total time
slope = rise / run = Δy / Δx
Inst. v = slope of tangent at a point
Avg. v = slope of secant line
Speed = |velocity| (always ≥ 0)
Part A — Calculation Problems (2 pts each)
A1

A cyclist travels 48 km in 3 hours. Calculate the average speed of the cyclist.

Given: d = 48 km, t = 3 h

A2

A car travels 200 km in 4 hours. What is its average speed in km/h?

Given: d = 200 km, t = 4 h

A3

On a position–time graph, two points are identified: (2 s, 3 m) and (8 s, 15 m). Find the average velocity.

Given: (t₁, x₁) = (2 s, 3 m); (t₂, x₂) = (8 s, 15 m)

A4

A runner's position–time graph shows points at (0 s, 0 m) and (10 s, 40 m). Calculate the average velocity over this interval.

Given: (t₁, x₁) = (0 s, 0 m); (t₂, x₂) = (10 s, 40 m)

A5

A tangent line drawn on a P–T graph at t = 3 s passes through the points (1 s, 2 m) and (5 s, 10 m). What is the instantaneous velocity at t = 3 s?

Tangent line passes through (1 s, 2 m) and (5 s, 10 m)

A6

The tangent to a P–T curve at t = 5 s passes through (3 s, 9 m) and (7 s, 1 m). What are the instantaneous velocity AND instantaneous speed at t = 5 s?

Tangent passes through (3 s, 9 m) and (7 s, 1 m)

Use the position–time graph below to answer the questions.

(a) Calculate the average velocity between the two green dots (t = 2 s to t = 8 s).

(b) What is the instantaneous velocity at the peak of the curve (t = 5 s)? Explain your reasoning.

(c) Is the instantaneous velocity positive, negative, or zero at t = 7 s? Explain.

C1

C2

A car's speedometer reads 85 km/h at a particular moment. Is this an average speed or instantaneous speed? Explain why.

C3

On a position–time graph, a section of the curve has a negative slope. What does this tell you about the object's (a) velocity and (b) speed at that point?

Answer Key — Classwork

A1 — Average speed of cyclist v̲ = d / t = 48 km / 3 h = 16 km/h
A2 — Average speed of car v̲ = 200 km / 4 h = 50 km/h
A3 — Average velocity from (2 s, 3 m) to (8 s, 15 m) v̲ = Δx / Δt = (15 − 3) / (8 − 2) = 12 / 6 = 2 m/s
A4 — Average velocity from (0 s, 0 m) to (10 s, 40 m) v̲ = (40 − 0) / (10 − 0) = 40 / 10 = 4 m/s
A5 — Instantaneous velocity at t = 3 s

Slope of tangent line through (1 s, 2 m) and (5 s, 10 m):

vᵢₙₛₜ = (10 − 2) / (5 − 1) = 8 / 4 = 2 m/s
A6 — Instantaneous velocity and speed at t = 5 s

Slope of tangent line through (3 s, 9 m) and (7 s, 1 m):

vᵢₙₛₜ = (1 − 9) / (7 − 3) = −8 / 4 = −2 m/s

Instantaneous speed = |−2| = 2 m/s (always positive magnitude)

B1 — Graph interpretation

(a) Both green dots are at x = 4.5 m, so Δx = 0. Δt = 8 − 2 = 6 s.

v̲ = 0 / 6 = 0 m/s

(b) At the peak, the tangent line is horizontal (slope = 0), so instantaneous velocity = 0 m/s. The object is momentarily at rest at the top of the curve before changing direction.

(c) At t = 7 s the curve is on the descending (right) side of the peak, so the slope is negative. Instantaneous velocity is negative (object moving in negative direction).

B2 — Tangent slopes table

(a)

t = 1 s: inst. velocity = +5 m/s; inst. speed = 5 m/s

t = 3 s: inst. velocity = 0 m/s; inst. speed = 0 m/s

t = 6 s: inst. velocity = −3 m/s; inst. speed = 3 m/s

(b) t = 6 s (negative velocity means the object is moving in the negative direction)

B3 — Tangent line at t = 4 s through (2 s, 1 m) and (6 s, 9 m) (a) vᵢₙₛₜ = (9 − 1) / (6 − 2) = 8 / 4 = +2 m/s

(b) Speed = |+2| = 2 m/s

(c) Positive slope → object is moving in the positive direction.

B4 — Average velocity vs. average speed (a) v̲ = Δx / Δt = (8 − 2) / (7 − 1) = 6 / 6 = 1 m/s (b) avg speed = total distance / time = 15 / 6 = 2.5 m/s

(c) Average speed uses total distance traveled along the path; average velocity uses displacement (straight-line change in position), so they differ when the path is curved or the object reverses direction.

C1 — Secant vs. Tangent

Average velocity is the slope of a secant line — a straight line connecting two specific points on the curve — while instantaneous velocity is the slope of the tangent line drawn at exactly one point on the curve.

C2 — Speedometer reading

It is instantaneous speed. A speedometer shows how fast the car is moving at that exact moment, not averaged over any time interval.

C3 — Negative slope on P–T graph

(a) Velocity: The instantaneous velocity is negative — the object is moving in the negative direction (backward/downward).

(b) Speed: The instantaneous speed is still positive (it is the magnitude of velocity), indicating the object is still moving.
