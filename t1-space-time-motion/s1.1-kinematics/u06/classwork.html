Kinematics · Unit 6

Velocity-Time Graphs I

Classwork — Constant Velocity V-T Graphs

Key Reference — Velocity-Time Graphs (Unit 6)

s = V × t   (displacement)
Horizontal line → constant velocity
Area above x-axis → +ve displacement
Area below x-axis → −ve displacement
V = 0 on x-axis → stationary
y-intercept → initial velocity (u)
V below x-axis → moving backwards
Total s = sum of all signed areas
PART A

Reading and Calculating — Basic Problems

A1 — DISPLACEMENT CALCULATION

An object travels at a constant velocity of +5 ms⁻¹ for 4 seconds. Calculate its displacement.

A2 — DISPLACEMENT CALCULATION

An object travels at a constant velocity of +3 ms⁻¹ for 5 seconds. What is its displacement?

A3 — NEGATIVE VELOCITY

An object moves at −2 ms⁻¹ for 6 seconds. Calculate its displacement. What does the negative sign tell you?

A4 — ZERO VELOCITY

An object is stationary (V = 0 ms⁻¹) for 7 seconds. How much displacement does it have during this interval? What does this segment look like on a v-t graph?

A5 — IDENTIFYING VELOCITY FROM GRAPH

A horizontal line on a v-t graph sits at V = +4 ms⁻¹. Is the object moving forward or backward? Is it accelerating? How do you know?

A6 — Y-INTERCEPT

A v-t graph has its line crossing the y-axis at V = +6 ms⁻¹. What does this tell you about the object's motion at t = 0? What is the initial velocity?

PART B

Graph-Based Problems — Area and Displacement

B1 — MULTI-SEGMENT DISPLACEMENT

Segment labels: Segment A (t = 1–4 s), Segment B (t = 4–6 s), Segment C (t = 6–10 s)

An object's v-t graph has two segments: V = +3 ms⁻¹ for 4 s, then V = −2 ms⁻¹ for 3 s. Calculate: (a) the displacement during each segment, and (b) the total displacement.

PART C

Short Answer — Concepts and Interpretation

C1 — CONCEPT

Explain in your own words why the area under a velocity-time graph gives displacement. Include the formula and the unit check (ms⁻¹ × s = m).

C2 — INTERPRETATION

A v-t graph has a segment that lies entirely below the x-axis. What does this mean for the object's motion and displacement? Can the distance travelled during this segment be zero? Explain.

C3 — DISTINGUISH

Two objects have v-t graphs. Object X has V = +4 ms⁻¹; Object Y has V = −4 ms⁻¹. Both travel for 5 seconds. Compare their displacements and their distances travelled. Are these the same or different? Explain.

C1

Displacement = velocity × time. On a v-t graph, a constant velocity segment forms a rectangle. Area = base × height = t × V = s. Unit check: ms⁻¹ × s = m (metres). Area above x-axis = positive s; area below = negative s.

s = V × t; area gives displacement, not distance.
C2

Below x-axis → negative velocity → object moves backward. Displacement is negative. Distance cannot be zero if V ≠ 0 — the object is moving, just in reverse, so distance is |s| = positive.

Negative displacement; distance is still positive (|s|).
C3

Object X: s = +4 × 5 = +20 m. Object Y: s = −4 × 5 = −20 m. Displacements are opposite (+20 vs −20). Distance is the same for both: 20 m (distance is always the magnitude of displacement for constant velocity).

Displacement: +20 m vs −20 m; Distance: both 20 m.
