Velocity-Time Graphs Part 2
Graphs of Changing Velocity

Key Formulae — VT Graphs

a = ΔV / Δt
Area (▲) = ½ × b × h
Area (▭) = b × h
Y-intercept = U (initial V)
Slope = acceleration
Area under graph = displacement

Part B — True / False

TF1
FALSE. The slope of a VT graph equals the acceleration, not the distance travelled. The area under the graph equals displacement.
TF2
TRUE. A horizontal line means ΔV = 0, so a = ΔV/Δt = 0.
TF3
FALSE. Area below the time axis represents negative displacement. It should be subtracted when calculating total displacement.
TF4
TRUE. The y-intercept is the value of V when t = 0, which is the initial velocity U = +5 ms⁻¹.
