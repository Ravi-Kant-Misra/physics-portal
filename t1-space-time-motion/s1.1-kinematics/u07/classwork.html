Answer Key — Classwork Unit 7

Part A — Accelerations

A1
a = +2 ms⁻²
ΔV = 6−0 = 6 ms⁻¹ | Δt = 3 s | a = 6/3 = +2 ms⁻²
A2
a = −2 ms⁻²
ΔV = 2−10 = −8 ms⁻¹ | Δt = 4 s | a = −8/4 = −2 ms⁻²
A3
a = 0 ms⁻²
Horizontal line → ΔV = 0 → a = 0/5 = 0
A4
a = +2 ms⁻²
ΔV = 5−(−3) = +8 ms⁻¹ | Δt = 4 s | a = 8/4 = +2 ms⁻²
A5
a = −2 ms⁻² — decelerating (slowing down in positive direction)
ΔV = 0−12 = −12 ms⁻¹ | Δt = 6 s | a = −12/6 = −2 ms⁻²
A6
P: Positive acceleration (slope upward)  |  Q: Zero acceleration (horizontal)  |  R: Negative acceleration (slope downward)

Part B — Displacement

B1
s = 20 m
Triangle: ½ × base × height = ½ × 5 × 8 = 20 m
B2
s = 28 m
Rectangle: base × height = (9−2) × 4 = 7 × 4 = 28 m
B3
Total s = 42 m
Triangle: ½ × 4 × 6 = 12 m | Rectangle: 5 × 6 = 30 m | Total = 12+30 = 42 m
B4
s = 50 m
Time to stop: Δt = ΔV/a = 20/4 = 5 s | Triangle area = ½ × 5 × 20 = 50 m

Part C — Short Answer

C1
The area under a VT graph gives displacement because displacement is a vector — it includes direction. Areas above the axis (positive velocity) count as positive displacement; areas below (negative velocity) subtract. Distance would require taking the magnitude of all areas, ignoring sign.
C2
Area below the axis represents negative displacement — the object is moving in the negative direction. It should be subtracted from (counted as negative in) the total displacement. For total distance it would be added as a positive value.
C3
The first section has a greater acceleration because it has a steeper slope. On a VT graph, steeper slope = greater magnitude of acceleration (a = ΔV/Δt, larger ΔV for same Δt means larger a).
