Kinematics · Unit 15

Classwork — Air Resistance on Projectiles

Apply your understanding of air drag, terminal velocity, and trajectory effects.

Key Formulas & Concepts

ΣF = m · a (Newton's 2nd Law)
F_air opposes velocity direction
g = 9.81 m/s² (down positive)
Terminal velocity: ΣF = 0 N
At terminal V: F_air = F_g
At terminal V: a = 0 m/s²
Slope of V-t graph = acceleration
Air resistance ∝ speed

Answer Key

Part A — Answers

01
a = F/m = 0.45 / 0.15 = 3.0 m/s² deceleration
Using Newton's 2nd Law: ΣFx = m · ax. The air resistance force is 0.45 N opposing motion. a = 0.45 / 0.15 = 3.0 m/s² (decelerating, opposite to direction of motion).
02
F_air = 0.462 N
ΣFy = m · ay → Fg − Fair = m · ay
Fg = mg = 0.20 × 9.81 = 1.962 N
1.962 − Fair = 0.20 × 7.50 = 1.50 N
Fair = 1.962 − 1.50 = 0.462 N
03
F_air = 4.905 N; acceleration = 0 m/s²
At terminal velocity, ΣF = 0 N → Fair = Fg = mg = 0.50 × 9.81 = 4.905 N. Since ΣF = 0, acceleration = 0 m/s².
04
Net force = 414.2 N upward; a = 5.18 m/s² upward
Before parachute: at terminal velocity, Fair,old = Fg = 80 × 9.81 = 784.8 N.
After parachute opens: Fair,new = 1200 N (upward), Fg = 784.8 N (downward).
ΣF = 1200 − 784.8 = 415.2 N upward (note: using exact value mg = 784.8 N)
a = ΣF / m = 415.2 / 80 = 5.19 m/s² upward (decelerating)
05
V_x ≈ 12.5 m/s
Using V = V0 + a·t (constant deceleration estimate):
Vx = 20 − (2.5)(3) = 20 − 7.5 = 12.5 m/s
(Note: with real air resistance the deceleration decreases as speed drops, so actual Vx would be slightly higher than this estimate.)
06
Answer: (a) Ball A lands farther than Ball B.
Ball A (vacuum) has no air resistance, so Vx is constant throughout. Ball B (air) has Fair continuously reducing both Vx and Vy components, resulting in a lower peak height, shorter horizontal range, and asymmetric trajectory (steeper descent).

Part B — Graph Interpretation Answers

07a
ay at t = 0 is 9.81 m/s². At t = 0 the ball is at rest, so V = 0 and therefore Fair = 0. The only force is gravity: a = g = 9.81 m/s².
07b
As time increases, ay decreases (the curve decays). As the ball speeds up, Fair increases (opposing motion), reducing the net downward force ΣF = Fg − Fair. Less net force → less acceleration.
07c
The acceleration approaches 0 m/s². This means Fair → Fg, so ΣF → 0. The ball is approaching terminal velocity — velocity becomes constant.
08
Vy vs t: Starts at 0 m/s. The curve rises with a concave-down (decreasing gradient) shape. It asymptotically approaches a horizontal dashed line = terminal velocity. The slope at any point = instantaneous acceleration (starts steep at 9.81, flattens to 0 at terminal velocity).
09
ay vs t: Starts at 9.81, decays to 0 (terminal velocity 1). At parachute opening: spikes sharply negative (large upward net force). Rapidly returns to 0 (new terminal velocity 2).
Vy vs t: Rises to TV1 (upper dashed line, flat). At parachute opening: drops sharply. Rises back to TV2 (lower dashed line, flat). TV2 < TV1.
10
A (no air resistance): Symmetric parabola, higher peak, longer range, Sx first half = Sx second half.
B (with air resistance): Lower peak, shorter range, asymmetric — descent side is steeper than ascent side. Vx arrows decrease along the path.

Part C — Short Answer Answers

11
Self-diminishing deceleration: Air resistance force is proportional to speed. When the ball is moving fast, Fair is large → large deceleration (ΣFx = m·ax). As the ball slows down, Fair decreases → the deceleration becomes smaller. So the ball slows down at a decreasing rate — never reaching zero velocity from air resistance alone (approaches zero asymptotically). This creates an exponential-style decay in speed.
12
The student is incorrect. Air resistance acts opposite to the velocity vector, which for a projectile is not purely horizontal (except at peak). As the ball travels through the air, its velocity vector has both Vx and Vy components. Fair opposes the total velocity vector, so it reduces both components — including Vy on the way up and on the way down. The trajectory description: "takes away from velocity in both the x and y directions."
13
Terminal velocity condition: ΣF = 0 → Fair = Fg = mg. With a parachute, the cross-sectional area is much larger, producing a much greater Fair at any given speed. To reach the condition Fair = Fg, the skydiver needs to be moving at a much lower speed (since Fair is already large even at low speeds). Therefore, the terminal velocity with a parachute is lower than without one.
