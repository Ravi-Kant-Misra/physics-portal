Homework — Air Resistance on Projectiles

Full working solutions required for Part A. Show all steps clearly.

Key Formulas & Concepts

ΣF = m · a
F_g = m · g (g = 9.81 m/s²)
Terminal V: ΣF = 0 → F_air = F_g
F_air opposes velocity direction
At terminal V: a = 0 m/s²
Slope of V-t = acceleration
Down is positive convention
Air resistance ∝ speed
Study Tip:

For all Part A problems, identify the known forces, apply ΣF = m·a, and check whether ΣF = 0 (terminal velocity) or ΣF ≠ 0 (still accelerating/decelerating). Show your formula, substitution, and final answer with units.

Answer Key

Part A — Full Working Answers

01
F_air = 0.903 N
ΣFy = m · ay (down positive)
Fg − Fair = m · ay
Fg = mg = 0.25 × 9.81 = 2.4525 N
Fair = Fg − m·ay = 2.4525 − (0.25 × 6.20) = 2.4525 − 1.55 = 0.9025 ≈ 0.903 N
02
(a) F_air,before = 588.6 N; (b) ΣF = 1211.4 N upward; (c) a = 20.19 m/s² upward
(a) At terminal velocity: Fair = Fg = mg = 60 × 9.81 = 588.6 N
(b) After parachute: ΣF = Fair,new − Fg = 1800 − 588.6 = 1211.4 N upward
(c) a = ΣF / m = 1211.4 / 60 = 20.19 m/s² upward (decelerating the fall)
03
(a) a = 5.0 m/s²; (b) a = 2.5 m/s² at half speed
(a) ΣFx = m · ax → a = Fair/m = 0.70 / 0.14 = 5.0 m/s² deceleration
(b) At 12.5 m/s (half speed), Fair is halved → Fair = 0.35 N
a = 0.35 / 0.14 = 2.5 m/s² — half the deceleration. This demonstrates the self-diminishing nature of air resistance.
04
(a) Lower; (b) F_g = 3.924 N; (c) ΣF = 0.576 N upward
(a) Terminal velocity in water is lower. In water, the combined resistive force (4.5 N) is greater than Fg at the speed the ball was travelling in air. The ball must slow to a speed where Fdrag,water = Fg = 3.924 N at the new, lower terminal velocity.
(b) Fg = mg = 0.40 × 9.81 = 3.924 N
(c) Upon entering water at air terminal velocity: Fdrag,water = 4.5 N up, Fg = 3.924 N down.
ΣF = 4.5 − 3.924 = 0.576 N upward (decelerating)
05
(a) 68 m; (b) 52 m reduction; (c) lower peak height / asymmetric trajectory (steeper descent)
(a) Total range with air resistance = 40 + 28 = 68 m
(b) Reduction = 120 − 68 = 52 m
(c) Accept any one of: lower peak height; trajectory is asymmetric (steeper on descent than ascent); Vx is continuously decreasing; landing speed is less than launch speed.
06
(a) F_g = 19.62 N; (b) F_air = 11.62 N; (c) F_air = 19.62 N, a = 0 m/s²
(a) Fg = mg = 2.0 × 9.81 = 19.62 N
(b) At t1: ΣF = m·a = 2.0 × 4.0 = 8.0 N (downward)
Fg − Fair = 8.0 → Fair = 19.62 − 8.0 = 11.62 N
(c) At terminal velocity: Fair = Fg = 19.62 N; acceleration = 0 m/s²

Part B — True/False Answers

07
FALSE. Correct statement: With air resistance, the trajectory is asymmetric — the second half of the range is shorter than the first half because Vx is continuously reduced by air drag. The descent is steeper than the ascent.
08
FALSE. Correct statement: At terminal velocity, ΣF = 0 N and therefore acceleration = 0 m/s² — but the velocity is constant and non-zero (it is the maximum falling speed). Zero net force means constant velocity, not zero velocity.
09
TRUE. The slope of the Vy vs t graph at any point equals the instantaneous acceleration at that time. As the curve flattens (slope → 0), the acceleration approaches 0 at terminal velocity.
10
FALSE. Correct statement: Air resistance acts opposite to the velocity vector, which has both horizontal (Vx) and vertical (Vy) components during flight. Therefore air resistance reduces both components — it "takes away from the velocity in both the x and y directions."

Part C — Challenge Answers

11a
Terminal velocity = 19.62 m/s
Terminal velocity condition: Fair = Fg
k·v = mg → 0.15·v = 0.30 × 9.81 = 2.943 N
v = 2.943 / 0.15 = 19.62 m/s
11b
At v = 10 m/s: F_air = 1.5 N; F_g = 2.943 N; ΣF = 1.443 N downward; a_y = 4.81 m/s²
Fair = k·v = 0.15 × 10 = 1.5 N
Fg = mg = 0.30 × 9.81 = 2.943 N
ΣF = Fg − Fair = 2.943 − 1.5 = 1.443 N downward
ay = ΣF / m = 1.443 / 0.30 = 4.81 m/s² downward
11c
Yes, the ball is still accelerating at v = 10 m/s. Because ΣF = 1.443 N ≠ 0, the net force is not zero, so the ball still has a downward acceleration of 4.81 m/s². Terminal velocity has not yet been reached (19.62 m/s).
12a
F_air,1 = 735.75 N; F_air,2 = 735.75 N
At both terminal velocities: ΣF = 0 → Fair = Fg = mg = 75 × 9.81 = 735.75 N
Note: At terminal velocity, Fair always equals Fg regardless of the speed. The difference is that the parachute produces the same opposing force at a much lower speed.
12b
Ratio = 1 (F_air,2 / F_air,1 = 735.75 / 735.75 = 1)
At any terminal velocity, Fair = Fg = constant. The ratio is always 1. The insight is that the parachute achieves the same Fair = Fg equilibrium at a far lower speed — the same force is generated at a much lower velocity due to the much greater surface area and drag coefficient of the parachute.
12c
Vy vs t graph shape:
1. Starts at 0 m/s, rises with decreasing gradient (concave-down curve).
2. Asymptotes to TV1 = 55 m/s (upper horizontal dashed line labeled "Terminal velocity without parachute").
3. Flat at TV1 until parachute opens.
4. At parachute opening: sharp drop in Vy.
5. Rises with decreasing gradient to TV2 = 6 m/s (lower horizontal dashed line labeled "Terminal velocity with parachute").
6. Flat at TV2 thereafter.
