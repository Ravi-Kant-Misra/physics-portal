Part C — Wire Field Diagrams

Problem 11 — Current UPWARD (out of page):
Left side: all ⊗ (field goes INTO the page)  |  Right side: all ⊙ (field comes OUT of the page)
Problem 12 — Current DOWNWARD (into page):
Left side: all ⊙ (field comes OUT of the page)  |  Right side: all ⊗ (field goes INTO the page)
Everything flips when the current reverses!

Part D — Short Answer

Problem 13: The ⊙ symbol represents a vector coming OUT of the page (toward the viewer). Using the arrow analogy: if an arrow is flying straight toward you, all you see is the arrowhead — a point (dot). The dot inside the circle in ⊙ represents that arrowhead pointing at your face.
Problem 14: Accept any correct magnetic field example. Best answer: In IB Physics, when we study magnetic fields around current-carrying wires, the field on one side of the wire points into the page and on the other side it points out of the page. Without 3D notation (⊙ and ⊗), we cannot represent these directions on a flat diagram.
Problem 15: x-axis = left-right across the screen surface; y-axis = up-down on the screen surface; z-axis = in/out of the screen (perpendicular to the screen — z comes toward you or goes away from you through the screen).
Problem 16 (Challenge): The current is flowing UPWARD out of the page. The pattern ⊗ on the left and ⊙ on the right exactly matches what we see with current flowing upward (out of the page) through a wire at the center.
