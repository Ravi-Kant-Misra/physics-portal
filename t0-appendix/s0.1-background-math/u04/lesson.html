Reducing Derived Units

Break down every named unit in physics into the 7 fundamental SI building blocks

🧩

What is a Derived Unit?

There are only 7 fundamental SI units (kg, m, s, K, mol, A, cd). Every other unit in physics — Newtons, Joules, Watts, Pascals, Volts, Ohms — is a derived unit: a combination of fundamental units multiplied or divided together.

To reduce a derived unit, you substitute the formula that defines it, then simplify using the rules of unit algebra — exactly like substituting variables in an algebraic expression.

This lecture assumes you are familiar with the main IB physics variables (force, energy, power, pressure, voltage, resistance).

The Reduction Method — 3 Steps

  1. Write the defining formula for the unit (e.g. F = ma)
  2. Substitute the units of each quantity on the right-hand side
  3. Simplify using unit algebra (multiply, divide, apply exponents)

The 6 Key Derived Units

💪
Newton (N)
Unit of Force
F = ma N = kg × m·s⁻² N = kg·m·s⁻²
1 N = kg·m·s⁻²
mass (kg) × acceleration (m·s⁻²) gives units of force
🌊
Pascal (Pa)
Unit of Pressure
p = F / Area Pa = (kg·m·s⁻²) / m² Pa = kg·m⁻¹·s⁻²
1 Pa = kg·m⁻¹·s⁻²
pressure = force ÷ area; m÷m² = m⁻¹
⚡
Joule (J)
Unit of Energy
Eₖ = ½mv² → J = kg·(m·s⁻¹)² E_g = mgh → J = kg·m·s⁻²·m J = kg·m²·s⁻²
1 J = kg·m²·s⁻²
two routes to the same result — both give kg·m²·s⁻²
💡
Watt (W)
Unit of Power
P = ΔE / t W = kg·m²·s⁻² / s W = kg·m²·s⁻³
1 W = kg·m²·s⁻³
energy (J) ÷ time (s): s⁻²÷s = s⁻³
🔋
Volt (V)
Unit of Voltage (EMF)
V = Ed (V = IR also works) V = (N/C)(m) = (kg·m·s⁻²/A·s)(m) V = kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹
1 V = kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹
current (A) appears as A⁻¹ because it's in the denominator
🔌
Ohm (Ω)
Unit of Resistance
R = V/I Ω = (kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻¹) / A Ω = kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻²
1 Ω = kg·m²·s⁻³·A⁻²
also: R = P/I² → kg·m²·s⁻³ / A² gives same result

Pattern to notice

Every derived unit is built from the same small set of fundamental units. Notice how kg·m²·s⁻² (the Joule) appears inside the Watt, Volt, and Ohm — because energy underlies all of them. Spotting these patterns is the key to remembering the reductions.
