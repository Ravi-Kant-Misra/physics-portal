Classwork — Chaining Uncertainty Rules

4 multi-step problems combining Rules 1, 2, and 3 in real physics contexts

Rule 1 — Add/Subtract
y = a ± b
Δy = Δa + Δb
(add ABSOLUTE uncert.)
Rule 2 — Multiply/Divide
y = (a×b)/c
Δy/y = Δa/a + Δb/b + Δc/c
(add FRACTIONAL uncert.)
Rule 3 — Powers & Roots
y = an
Δy/y = |n × Δa/a|
(multiply frac. uncert. by |n|)

Surface Area of a Sphere
Rule 3 Powers
A sphere has radius r = 8.0 cm ± 0.4 cm. The surface area is A = 4πr².
(a) What is the percentage uncertainty in r?
(b) What is the percentage uncertainty in A?
(c) If A = 804 cm², what is the absolute uncertainty in A?
r = 8.0 ± 0.4 cm
A = 4πr²
4π = exact constant

✅ Full Solution

a
%uncert(r) = (Δr/r) × 100 = (0.4/8.0) × 100 = 5%
b
A = 4πr². The constant 4π is exact (zero uncertainty). Apply Rule 3 with n = 2:
%uncert(A) = |2| × %uncert(r) = 2 × 5% = 10%
c
Absolute uncertainty: ΔA = 10% × 804 = 0.10 × 804 = 80.4 cm² → round to ΔA ≈ 80 cm²
A = 804 ± 80 cm²  (10% uncertainty)

Kinetic Energy
Rule 3 Rule 2 Two-stage chain
A moving object has mass m = 2.0 kg ± 5% and velocity v = 10.0 m/s ± 3%. The kinetic energy is KE = ½mv².
(a) What is the percentage uncertainty in v²?
(b) What is the percentage uncertainty in KE?
(c) If KE = 100 J, state KE with its absolute uncertainty.
m: 5% uncert.
v: 3% uncert.
KE = ½mv²
½ = exact

(a) %uncert(v²) =
(b) %uncert(KE) =
(c) KE = 100 J ±

✅ Full Solution

a
v²: Apply Rule 3 with n = 2. %uncert(v²) = 2 × 3% = 6%
b
KE = ½ × m × v². Constant ½ is exact. Apply Rule 2 (multiply m and v²):
%uncert(KE) = %uncert(m) + %uncert(v²) = 5% + 6% = 11%
c
Absolute uncertainty: ΔKE = 11% × 100 J = 11 J
KE = 100 ± 11 J  (11% uncertainty)

Period of a Simple Pendulum
Rule 3 — square root Rule 2
The period of a simple pendulum is T = 2π√(L/g). The length is L = 1.00 m ± 2% and gravitational acceleration g = 9.81 m/s² is treated as exact (no uncertainty).
(a) What is the percentage uncertainty in L/g?
(b) What is the percentage uncertainty in √(L/g)?
(c) What is the percentage uncertainty in T?
L: 2% uncert.
g: exact (0%)
T = 2π√(L/g)
2π = exact

✅ Full Solution

a
L/g: g is exact (0%). Rule 2: %uncert(L/g) = %uncert(L) + %uncert(g) = 2% + 0% = 2%
b
Square root = power n = ½. Rule 3: %uncert(√(L/g)) = ½ × 2% = 1%
c
T = 2π × √(L/g). Constant 2π is exact. So:
%uncert(T) = %uncert(√(L/g)) = 1%
%uncertainty in T = 1%

Pressure Inside a Gas Tank
Rule 3 Rule 2 Rule 1 All three rules
The pressure of a gas is given by P = nRT / V, where:
• The number of moles: n = 2.00 mol ± 1%
• The gas constant R = 8.314 J/(mol·K) is exact
• Temperature: T = 300 K ± 5 K
• Volume: V = 0.050 m³ ± 4%
(a) Find %uncert(T).  (b) Find %uncert(nRT).  (c) Find %uncert(P).
n: 1% uncert.
R: exact
T = 300 ± 5 K
V: 4% uncert.

(a) %uncert(T) =
(b) %uncert(nRT) =
(c) %uncert(P) =

✅ Full Solution

a
Convert absolute uncertainty in T to fractional:
%uncert(T) = (ΔT/T) × 100 = (5/300) × 100 ≈ 1.67%
b
nRT = n × R × T. R is exact. Apply Rule 2 (multiply):
%uncert(nRT) = %uncert(n) + %uncert(T) = 1% + 1.67% = 2.67%
c
P = nRT / V. Apply Rule 2 (division):
%uncert(P) = %uncert(nRT) + %uncert(V) = 2.67% + 4% = 6.67% ≈ 6.7%
%uncertainty in P ≈ 6.7%
