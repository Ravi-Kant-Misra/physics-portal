Part B
P11. Step 1 (add): 6.4 + 1.20 = 7.60 → 1 dp limit (6.4) → use 7.6 for sig fig count (2 sf), carry 7.60.
Step 2 (multiply): 7.60 × 2.50 = 19.0 → 2.50 has 3 sf, 7.60 treated as 2 sf → 2 sf → 19
P12. Step 1 (subtract): 18.5 − 3.20 = 15.30 → 18.5 has 1 dp (fewest) → use 15.3 (3 sf), carry 15.30.
Step 2 (divide): 15.30 ÷ 4.0 = 3.825 → 4.0 has 2 sf (fewest) → 3.8
P13. Area = 4.53 × 2.3 = 10.419 m². 4.53 has 3 sf, 2.3 has 2 sf → 2 sf. Answer: 10 m²
Part C
Q14. Mass of liquid = 82.45 − 50.0 = 32.45 g. This is subtraction, so count decimal places: 50.0 has 1 dp (fewest), 82.45 has 2 dp. Answer must have 1 dp → 32.4 g. The rule used is the addition/subtraction rule: round to the fewest decimal places.
Q15. Speed = 125.0 ÷ 2.5 = 50.0 km/h. Division uses sig figs: 125.0 has 4 sf, 2.5 has 2 sf → 2 sf. 50.0 rounded to 2 sf = 50 km/h. The answer has 2 sig figs because 2.5 (the time) was the least precise measurement.
Bonus
Bonus. No, 3.80 g/cm³ is not correct. Division uses sig figs: 45.6 g has 3 sf, 12 cm³ has only 2 sf — so the answer must have 2 sig figs. 45.6 ÷ 12 = 3.8 g/cm³. The student's answer of 3.80 falsely claims 3 sig figs of precision, but the volume was only measured to 2 sig figs. Correct answer: 3.8 g/cm³.
