The 3 Steps (Factor-Label Method)

  1. Write the unit you want to get rid of in the denominator of your conversion fraction
  2. Write the unit you want to end up with in the numerator
  3. Fill in numbers so that numerator value = denominator value (making the fraction = 1)

Part C — Short Answer

8. A student converts 500 m to km and writes: 500 m × (1000 km / 1 m) = 500,000 km. Identify and explain the error. What is the correct answer?
9. Explain in your own words why multiplying by a conversion factor does not change the actual physical quantity.
10. A car's speed is 25 m/s. Convert this to km/h. Show your conversion factors clearly.

Answer Key

Part A — Single-Step

Q1: 4500 m → km
4500 × (1 km/1000 m) = 4.5 km
Q2: 2.5 h → s
2.5 × (60 min/1h) × (60 s/1 min) = 9000 s
Q3: 850 cm → m
850 × (1 m/100 cm) = 8.50 m
Q4: 5.0 kg → g
5.0 × (1000 g/1 kg) = 5000 g

Part B — Multi-Step

Q5: 108 km/h → m/s
108 × (1000m/1km) × (1h/60min) × (1min/60s) = 30 m/s
Q6: 1 year → s
365 × 24 × 60 × 60 = 31,536,000 s ≈ 3.15 × 10⁷ s
Q7: 3.0 Mm → mm
3.0 Mm × (10⁶ m/1 Mm) × (1 mm/10⁻³ m) = 3.0 × 10⁹ mm

Part C — Short Answer

Q8: The student put km on top and m on bottom in the wrong arrangement. The km unit does not cancel — instead it creates km²/m. The old unit (m) must go on the bottom so it cancels with the original m. Correct: 500 m × (1 km / 1000 m) = 0.5 km.
Q9: Each conversion factor equals exactly 1, because the numerator and denominator represent the same physical quantity (just in different units). Multiplying any number by 1 leaves the value unchanged. The unit changes, but the physical measurement does not.
Q10: 25 m/s × (1 km / 1000 m) × (60 s / 1 min) × (60 min / 1 h) = 25 × 3600 / 1000 = 90 km/h
