Answer Key

Part A

Q1: 7.2×10⁴ cm → km
7.2×10⁴ × (1m/100cm) × (1km/1000m) = 0.72 km
Q2: 540 km/h → m/s
540 × (1000/1) × (1/3600) = 150 m/s
Q3: 5 weeks → s
5 × 7 × 24 × 3600 = 3,024,000 s ≈ 3.02×10⁶ s
Q4: 1.5×10⁻⁶ m → nm
1.5×10⁻⁶ × (1nm/10⁻⁹ m) = 1500 nm
Q5: 72 m/s² → km/h²
72 × (1km/1000m) × (3600s/1h)² = 72 × 10⁻³ × 1.296×10⁷ = 933,120 km/h² ≈ 9.33×10⁵ km/h²
The s² means (s→h conversion) must be applied twice: ×(3600)²

Part B — True/False

Q6
FALSE
To convert m→cm, put cm on top: (100 cm / 1 m). The given fraction would go the wrong direction.
Q7
FALSE
Need at least 3 fractions: km→m, h→min, min→s (or combine h→s in two steps)
Q8
FALSE
Conversion factors equal 1; multiplying by 1 never changes the actual physical value

Part C — Extended

Q9: 3×10⁸ m/s × (1 km/1000 m) × (3600 s/1 h) = 3×10⁸ × 3.6 = 1.08 × 10⁹ km/h
Q10: 1 Mm × (10⁶ m / 1 Mm) × (1 mm / 10⁻³ m) = 10⁶ ÷ 10⁻³ = 10⁹ mm. So 1 Mm = 10⁹ mm, NOT 10³ mm. The student is wrong.
Q11: 3 schools × (4 grades/1 school) × (5 classes/1 grade) × (30 students/1 class) = 3 × 4 × 5 × 30 = 1800 students
