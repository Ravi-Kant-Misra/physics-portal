Answer Key

Part A — Relationships

Q1: W = Fd, W vs d
Linear (W ∝ d)
F = c; W = c·d → straight line
Q2: P = F²/2m, P vs F
Quadratic (P ∝ F²)
1/2m = c; P = c·F² → parabola
Q3: T vs L (pendulum)
T² ∝ L (linear); T ∝ √L
T² = (4π²/g)·L → T² vs L is linear; T vs L is a square-root curve
Q4: F = kq₁q₂/r², F vs r
Inverse Square (F ∝ 1/r²)
kq₁q₂ = c; F = c/r² → steep drop

Part B — Predictions

Q5: v tripled in Eₖ = ½mv²
Eₖ × 9
Eₖ ∝ v²; (3v)² = 9v² → Eₖ multiplies by 9
Q6: r halved in g = Gm/r²
g × 4
g ∝ 1/r²; (r/2)² = r²/4 → g = c/(r²/4) = 4c/r² → g quadruples
Q7: q₁,q₂ doubled AND r doubled
Net: F × 1 (unchanged)
Charges: (2q₁)(2q₂) = 4q₁q₂ → F×4. Distance: (2r)² = 4r² → F÷4. Net: 4/4 = 1 → no change.

Part D — Extended

Q11: (a) F ∝ M — linear (straight line on F vs M graph). (b) F ∝ 1/r² — inverse square. (c) If M triples: F × 3. If r doubles: F ÷ 4. Net effect: F × 3/4 = 0.75× original F (F decreases by 25%).
Q12: From V = IR → R = V/I = c/I (V held constant). This is an inverse relationship (R ∝ 1/I). The graph will be a hyperbola — as current increases, resistance decreases proportionally, and the curve will never touch either axis. The physics: with fixed voltage, more resistance = less current flowing (and vice versa).
