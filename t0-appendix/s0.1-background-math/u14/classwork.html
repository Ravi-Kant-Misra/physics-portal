Part C & D

C1: Temperature / °C ± 0.5
C2: DV: Force (N) ± 0.2  [spanning Trial 1, Trial 2, Trial 3]
C3: Any three of: wire diameter, coil diameter, length of spring, spring material, temperature, type of mass used.
D4: Use the measurement uncertainty (±0.001 m) because it is the larger of the two (0.001 stat = 0.001 meas — equal, so either works, use 0.001). Error bar = ±0.001 m. The error bar is always the LARGER uncertainty.
D5: With only one trial per IV value, there is no spread to measure — you cannot calculate (max−min)/2 from a single data point. Statistical uncertainty requires at least two trials to have any meaning. The error bar then relies solely on measurement uncertainty, which may underestimate the true variability in the experiment.
