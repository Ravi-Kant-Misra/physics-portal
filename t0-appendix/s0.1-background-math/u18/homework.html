Formulas & Key Rules

  • Vector symbol: ⃗A (arrow overhead)  |  Scalar: A (plain letter)
  • Scalar mult.: positive scalar → same direction; negative → flip direction
  • Tip-to-tail: tail of 2nd vector at tip of 1st; resultant = tail of 1st to tip of last
  • Perpendicular: A² + B² = C²  |  Collinear (same dir.): add  |  Collinear (opp. dir.): subtract
  • Commutative: ⃗A + ⃗B = ⃗B + ⃗A (order doesn't change the resultant)

Answer Key

Part A — Concepts & Notation

Problem 1: ⃗A is a vector — it has both magnitude and direction (e.g., ⃗A = 10 m East). A is the scalar magnitude only (e.g., A = 10 m, no direction stated).
Problem 2: Magnitude = 36 m (|−3| × 12 = 36). Direction = left (West / opposite direction). Negative scalar multiplies magnitude and flips direction.
Problem 3: Answer = B — ⃗A + ⃗B = ⃗B + ⃗A. The commutative property holds for all vector addition.
Problem 4: a) TRUE — −1 × ⃗A = same magnitude, opposite direction.
b) FALSE — plain letter A gives only magnitude, not direction.
c) FALSE — two perpendicular vectors of magnitude M give resultant = M√2 (not M).

Part B — Drawing & Labeling

Problem 5: ⃗F = 9 N pointing left (west); 2⃗F = 18 N also pointing left, drawn twice as long.
Problem 6: ⃗V = 7 m/s up; −⃗V = 7 m/s pointing down (opposite direction). Same magnitude, reversed.
Problem 7 — ⃗C = 10 m East:
3⃗C: 30 m, East, tripled magnitude  |  0.25⃗C: 2.5 m, East, quarter magnitude  |  −3⃗C: 30 m, West, tripled + flipped  |  −0.5⃗C: 5 m, West, halved + flipped

Part D — Analysis

Problem 13: Resultant = 0 m. Equal and opposite vectors cancel. This shows that a vector and its negative completely cancel each other out.
Problem 14: The student is incorrect. Scalar addition gives 3+4=7 only when vectors are in the same direction. Perpendicular 3 m and 4 m vectors give 5 m (Pythagorean theorem). Opposite vectors give 1 m. The resultant depends on direction, not just magnitude.
Problem 15: 4² + 3² = 16+9 = 25 → C = 5 m/s. The swimmer's actual speed relative to the ground is 5 m/s (at an angle northeast).
