Review of Trig Sides

Identifying Sides • Calculating Ratios • Solving for Missing Sides

Quick Reference — SOH CAH TOA
Sin(θ) = Opp/Hyp Cos(θ) = Adj/Hyp Tan(θ) = Opp/Adj
📌

Part A — Identify the Sides

For each triangle diagram below, identify which side is the Opposite, Adjacent, and Hypotenuse for the marked angle θ. Type your answers (use: O, A, or H) in the boxes.

Q1 Angle θ at Bottom-Left — 3-4-5 Triangle

Which label (O, A, or H) belongs to Side P, Side Q, and Side R?

Side P (vertical, length 3): Opposite — it is directly across from θ and does not touch it.

Side Q (horizontal base, length 4): Adjacent — it sits next to θ and forms the base of the angle.

Side R (diagonal, length 5): Hypotenuse — the longest side, opposite the right angle.

Q2 Angle θ at Top-Right — Same 3-4-5 Triangle

Now θ is at the top-right. Which label (O, A, or H) belongs to Side P, Side Q, and Side R?

Side P (horizontal base, length 4): Opposite — it is now across from θ at the top-right.

Side Q (vertical right, length 3): Adjacent — it sits right next to θ.

Side R (diagonal, length 5): Hypotenuse — always the longest side, unchanged.

Note: Opposite and Adjacent have swapped compared to Q1 because the angle moved!

Part B — Calculate Trig Ratios

Use SOH-CAH-TOA to calculate Sin(θ), Cos(θ), and Tan(θ) as decimals (round to 2 decimal places).

Q3 5-12-13 Right Triangle — θ at Bottom-Left

Opposite = 5, Adjacent = 12, Hypotenuse = 13. Find all three trig ratios.

Sin(θ) = 5/13 ≈ 0.38

Cos(θ) = 12/13 ≈ 0.92

Tan(θ) = 5/12 ≈ 0.42

Q4 8-15-17 Right Triangle — θ at Bottom-Left

Opposite = 8, Adjacent = 15, Hypotenuse = 17. Find all three trig ratios.

Sin(θ) = 8/17 ≈ 0.47

Cos(θ) = 15/17 ≈ 0.88

Tan(θ) = 8/15 ≈ 0.53

Part C — Solve for Missing Sides

Use the 3-step method: (1) choose trig function, (2) rearrange, (3) calculate. Round answers to 1 decimal place.

Q5 Find the Opposite Side

A right triangle has angle θ = 40° and Adjacent = 30 m. Find the Opposite side (O).

Hint: Which function links Opposite and Adjacent?

Step 1: Know Adjacent, want Opposite → use Tan

Step 2: Tan(θ) = O/A  →  O = A × Tan(θ)

Step 3: O = 30 × Tan(40°) = 30 × 0.839 ≈ 25.2 m

Q6 Find the Hypotenuse

A right triangle has angle θ = 25° and Opposite = 18 m. Find the Hypotenuse (H).

Hint: Which function links Opposite and Hypotenuse?

Step 1: Know Opposite, want Hypotenuse → use Sin

Step 2: Sin(θ) = O/H  →  H = O / Sin(θ)

Step 3: H = 18 / Sin(25°) = 18 / 0.423 ≈ 42.6 m

Q7 Find the Adjacent Side

A right triangle has angle θ = 55° and Hypotenuse = 80 m. Find the Adjacent side (A).

Hint: Which function links Adjacent and Hypotenuse?

Step 1: Know Hypotenuse, want Adjacent → use Cos

Step 2: Cos(θ) = A/H  →  A = H × Cos(θ)

Step 3: A = 80 × Cos(55°) = 80 × 0.574 ≈ 45.9 m
