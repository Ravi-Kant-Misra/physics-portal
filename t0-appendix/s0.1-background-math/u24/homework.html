Part A — Identify the Sides

HW 1 6-8-10 Right Triangle — Angle θ at Bottom-Left

Identify each side relative to θ at the bottom-left. Use: Opposite (O), Adjacent (A), Hypotenuse (H).

Side X (vertical, length 6): Opposite — directly across from θ, does not touch it.

Side Y (horizontal, length 8): Adjacent — the base of angle θ, right next to it.

Side Z (diagonal, length 10): Hypotenuse — longest side, opposite the right angle.

HW 2 Same 6-8-10 Triangle — Angle θ at Top-Right Corner

Now θ is at the top-right corner. How do the labels change?

Side X (horizontal base, length 8): Opposite — now it is across from θ at the top-right.

Side Y (vertical right, length 6): Adjacent — now it sits right next to θ.

Side Z (diagonal, length 10): Hypotenuse — unchanged, always the longest side.

Note: Opposite (8) and Adjacent (6) have swapped from HW 1 because the angle moved!

Part C — Solve for Missing Sides

Use the 3-step method. Show your working in the work area. Round to 1 decimal place unless stated.

HW 5 Find the Adjacent Side

A right triangle has angle θ = 35° and Hypotenuse = 60 m. Find the Adjacent side (A).

Hint: Which function uses Adjacent and Hypotenuse?

Step 1: Know Hypotenuse, want Adjacent → use Cos

Step 2: Cos(θ) = A/H  →  A = H × Cos(θ)

Step 3: A = 60 × Cos(35°) = 60 × 0.819 ≈ 49.1 m

HW 6 Find the Opposite Side

A right triangle has angle θ = 62° and Hypotenuse = 45 m. Find the Opposite side (O).

Hint: Which function uses Opposite and Hypotenuse?

Step 1: Know Hypotenuse, want Opposite → use Sin

Step 2: Sin(θ) = O/H  →  O = H × Sin(θ)

Step 3: O = 45 × Sin(62°) = 45 × 0.883 ≈ 39.7 m

HW 7 Find the Hypotenuse

A right triangle has angle θ = 48° and Adjacent = 35 m. Find the Hypotenuse (H).

Hint: Which function uses Adjacent and Hypotenuse? How do you rearrange it?

Step 1: Know Adjacent, want Hypotenuse → use Cos

Step 2: Cos(θ) = A/H  →  H = A / Cos(θ)

Step 3: H = 35 / Cos(48°) = 35 / 0.669 ≈ 52.3 m

HW 8 Find the Opposite Side (Tan rearrangement)

A right triangle has angle θ = 72° and Adjacent = 12 m. Find the Opposite side (O).

Step 1: Know Adjacent, want Opposite → use Tan

Step 2: Tan(θ) = O/A  →  O = A × Tan(θ)

Step 3: O = 12 × Tan(72°) = 12 × 3.078 ≈ 36.9 m

Part D — Extended Problems

These problems require you to apply multiple steps or think carefully about which sides correspond to the angle given.

HW 9 — Find Both Missing Sides

A right triangle has one angle θ = 20° and the Hypotenuse = 100 m. Find both the Opposite and Adjacent sides.

Part (a): Find the Opposite side using Sin.

Part (b): Find the Adjacent side using Cos.

Part (a) — Opposite:

Sin(θ) = O/H → O = H × Sin(20°) = 100 × 0.342 ≈ 34.2 m

Part (b) — Adjacent:

Cos(θ) = A/H → A = H × Cos(20°) = 100 × 0.940 ≈ 94.0 m

Check: √(34.2² + 94.0²) = √(1169.6 + 8836) = √10005.6 ≈ 100 ✓

HW 10 — Conceptual Understanding

A right triangle has the same side lengths but you move angle θ from the bottom-left to the other non-right angle (top-right).

(a) Which sides change their labels (Opposite / Adjacent / Hypotenuse)?

(b) If originally Sin(θ) = 0.6, what does Sin become for the new angle? (Give your answer as a decimal.)

(c) What trig function for the new angle gives you 0.6? Explain why using SOH-CAH-TOA.

(a) The Opposite and Adjacent swap. The Hypotenuse never changes because it is always the side opposite the right angle, regardless of which other angle is called θ.

(b) If the original triangle had Sin(θ) = Opp/Hyp = 0.6, then when you move to the new angle, the old Opposite becomes the new Adjacent. So Sin(new θ) = new Opp / Hyp = old Adj / Hyp = Cos(old θ).

Since Cos(old θ) = Adj/Hyp and Sin + Cos complement each other in a right triangle: Sin(new θ) = 0.8 (if old triangle was 3-4-5: Cos = 4/5 = 0.8).

(c) Cos(new θ) = 0.6, because what was the Opposite (= 3) is now the Adjacent of the new angle, and Cos = Adj/Hyp. The two non-right angles are complementary (add to 90°), so Sin of one equals Cos of the other.
