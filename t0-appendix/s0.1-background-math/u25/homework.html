Part D — Analysis

15. The student multiplied both sides by H but forgot to then divide by Sin(θ). The correct rearrangement from Sin(θ) = O/H is: multiply both sides by H to get H·Sin(θ) = O, then divide both sides by Sin(θ) to get H = O/Sin(θ). Never multiply by H and multiply by Sin — that gives H = O×Sin, which is wrong.
16. O = H·Sin(30°) = 10 × 0.5 = 5. Sin(30°) = 0.5 exactly because in an equilateral triangle split in half, the short side is exactly half the hypotenuse — no rounding needed.
17. As θ increases toward 90°, Sin(θ) increases toward 1, so O = H·Sin(θ) increases toward H (the opposite side grows). Meanwhile Cos(θ) decreases toward 0, so A = H·Cos(θ) decreases toward 0 (the adjacent side shrinks). At 90° the triangle would be a vertical line — no adjacent side at all.
