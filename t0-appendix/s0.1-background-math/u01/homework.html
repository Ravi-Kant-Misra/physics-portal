Rules Reminder

  1. The farthest-left non-zero digit is the most significant figure.
  2. No decimal point → farthest-right non-zero digit is the least significant figure.
  3. Decimal point present → farthest-right digit is least significant — even if it is zero.
  4. All digits between most and least significant are significant.
Scientific notation tip: Only count digits in the coefficient. The power of 10 does not affect significant figures.

Part C

Q16. 1200 mm is ambiguous because there is no decimal point. It could be 2 sig figs (±50 mm) or 4 sig figs (±0.5 mm). To remove ambiguity use scientific notation: 1.2 × 10³ mm (2 sf), 1.20 × 10³ mm (3 sf), or 1.200 × 10³ mm (4 sf).

Q17. No. A stopwatch measuring to 0.1 s limits the precision of the speed result. Writing 12.347 m/s falsely claims 5 sig figs. The answer should be rounded to reflect the instrument's precision — likely 12.3 m/s (3 sig figs).

Bonus

Bonus. 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s has 3 sig figs. Rounding from 299,792,458 m/s drops six places of precision (~207,542 m/s lost). For everyday physics 3 sf is enough. For GPS synchronisation, interferometry, or testing physical constants, the full 9-digit precision is essential.
