Reference — The Rule

For multiplication (y = a × b):
Δy/y = Δa/a + Δb/b
For division (y = a ÷ b):
Δy/y = Δa/a + Δb/b  (same rule!)
Three steps:
1. Compute result (y)
2. Find fractional uncertainties (Δ/val) and add them
3. Multiply fractional × y to get absolute Δy
Percent uncertainty:
If given as %, add percentages directly.
Result % = %₁ + %₂
KEY CONTRAST: Adding/Subtracting → add ABSOLUTE uncertainties  |  Multiplying/Dividing → add FRACTIONAL (relative) uncertainties
Fractional uncertainties ALWAYS add Even for division — still ADD Convert absolute → fractional: Δa/a Do NOT add absolute uncertainties for ×÷

Answer Key

Part A — Multiplication

Problem 1
(4±1 m) × (6±2 m)
24 m² ± 0.58 (frac) → 24 ± 14 m²
y = 24. Δa/a = 1/4 = 0.25. Δb/b = 2/6 = 0.33. Total frac = 0.58. Absolute: 0.58 × 24 ≈ 14 m².
Problem 2
(3.0±0.6 kg) × (5.0±0.5 kg)
15 kg² ± 0.30 (frac) → 15 ± 4.5 kg²
y = 15. Δa/a = 0.6/3 = 0.20. Δb/b = 0.5/5 = 0.10. Total = 0.30. Absolute: 0.30 × 15 = 4.5 kg².
Problem 3
(15 N ±20%) × (8 N ±10%)
120 N² ± 30%
y = 15 × 8 = 120. Add percents: 20% + 10% = 30%. Absolute: 0.30 × 120 = 36 N².
Problem 4
(9.0±0.9 s) × (2.0±0.4 s)
18 s² ± 0.30 (frac) → 18 ± 5.4 s²
y = 18. Δa/a = 0.9/9 = 0.10. Δb/b = 0.4/2 = 0.20. Total = 0.30. Absolute: 0.30 × 18 = 5.4 s².

Part B — Division

Problem 5
(20±4 N) ÷ (5±1 m)
4 N/m ± 0.40 (frac) → 4 ± 1.6 N/m
y = 4. Δa/a = 4/20 = 0.20. Δb/b = 1/5 = 0.20. Total = 0.40. Absolute: 0.40 × 4 = 1.6.
Problem 6
(36±6 J) ÷ (12±3 s)
3 W ± 0.42 (frac) → 3 ± 1.25 W
y = 3. Δa/a = 6/36 = 0.167. Δb/b = 3/12 = 0.25. Total = 0.417 ≈ 0.42. Absolute: 0.42 × 3 ≈ 1.25 W.
Problem 7
(100 W ±15%) ÷ (50 V ±5%)
2.0 A ± 20%
y = 2. Add percents: 15% + 5% = 20%. Absolute: 0.20 × 2 = 0.4 A. So 2.0 ± 0.4 A.
Problem 8
(8.4±0.6 m) ÷ (2.1±0.3 s)
4.0 m/s ± 0.214 (frac) → 4.0 ± 0.86 m/s
y = 4. Δa/a = 0.6/8.4 ≈ 0.071. Δb/b = 0.3/2.1 ≈ 0.143. Total ≈ 0.214. Absolute: 0.214 × 4 ≈ 0.86 m/s.

Part D — Short Answer

Q13: Area = 8.0 × 3.0 = 24.0 m². Frac. uncs: 0.4/8.0 = 5%, 0.3/3.0 = 10%. Total = 15%. Absolute: 0.15 × 24 = 3.6 m². Width contributes more (10% vs 5%) because its uncertainty is larger relative to its size. To improve the result, reduce the width measurement uncertainty first.
Q14: Mistake: added absolute uncertainties (3+1=4) instead of using fractional rule. Correct: y = 48 N. Δa/a = 3/12 = 0.25. Δb/b = 1/4 = 0.25. Total = 0.50. Absolute: 0.50 × 48 = 24 N. Answer: 48 ± 24 N (not 48 ± 4 N).
Q15: Option A: v = 10 m/s. Frac: 5/100 = 0.05, 0.5/10 = 0.05. Total = 0.10 (10%). Absolute: 1.0 m/s. Option B: v = 10 m/s. Frac: 1/100 = 0.01, 2/10 = 0.20. Total = 0.21 (21%). Absolute: 2.1 m/s. Option A is far more reliable (10% vs 21%). In Option B, the poor time measurement (2 s uncertainty) dominates and makes the result twice as uncertain. Better timing equipment is the priority.
