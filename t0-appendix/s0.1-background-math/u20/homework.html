Part C — Conceptual Questions

Question 9 Explain the reasoning
Conceptual
A student uses tan⁻¹(horizontal/vertical) to find an angle "from horizontal". Is this correct? Explain why or why not and state the correct formula.
Answer space (write or type your reasoning)...
Incorrect. The angle is from horizontal, so horizontal = adjacent and vertical = opposite.
Correct formula: θ = tan⁻¹(vertical / horizontal) = tan⁻¹(opposite / adjacent). Swapping gives the complement of the desired angle (90° − θ), which measures from the vertical instead.
Question 10 Challenge — when are all three formulas equivalent?
Challenge
If a right triangle has a vertical component of 5 m and a horizontal component of 5 m, calculate the angle using all three inverse trig functions (you will need to find the hypotenuse first). What do you notice?
Work space...
Hypotenuse = √(5²+5²) = √50 = 5√2 ≈ 7.07 m
sin⁻¹(5/7.07) = sin⁻¹(0.707) = 45°
cos⁻¹(5/7.07) = cos⁻¹(0.707) = 45°
tan⁻¹(5/5) = tan⁻¹(1) = 45°
All three give 45°! This happens because when the two legs are equal, the triangle is an isosceles right triangle. sin and cos give the same answer because sin(45°) = cos(45°) = 1/√2.
