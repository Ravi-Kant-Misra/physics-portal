Finding the Angle — Classwork

Practice applying inverse trig to find resultant directions

Inverse Sine
sin⁻¹(opp/hyp) = θ
Inverse Cosine
cos⁻¹(adj/hyp) = θ
Inverse Tangent
tan⁻¹(opp/adj) = θ
Key Rule
opp = vertical, adj = horiz
📝

Instructions: For each problem, identify the given sides, choose the correct inverse trig formula, show your calculation, and state the angle. Always specify the direction (e.g. North of East, South of West). Round to 1 decimal place.

A

Part A — Find the Angle

Problem 1 tan⁻¹ — Both legs given

A displacement vector has a horizontal component of 10 m (East) and a vertical component of 5 m (North). Find the angle of the resultant from horizontal.

Horizontal (adj) = 10 m Vertical (opp) = 5 m

B

Part B — Real-World Scenarios

Scenario 1 Navigation — Wind + Plane

A small plane flies North at 120 km/h. A wind blows East at 35 km/h. Find:

(a) The resultant speed of the plane: √(120² + 35²) = √(14400 + 1225) = √15625 = 125 km/h
(b) The angle of the resultant from horizontal (North = vertical, East = horizontal): θ = tan⁻¹(35 / 120)
θ =
θ = tan⁻¹(35/120) = tan⁻¹(0.292)
θ = 16.3° East of North

The plane travels at 125 km/h, 16.3° East of North

Scenario 2 Ramp Force

A box is pushed with a force that has a horizontal component of 18 N (East) and a vertical component of 24 N (North). Find the magnitude and angle of the resultant force from horizontal.

Magnitude: R = √(18² + 24²) = √(324 + 576) = √900 = 30 N
θ =
θ = tan⁻¹(24/18) = tan⁻¹(1.333)
θ = 53.1° North of East

Resultant: 30 N at 53.1° North of East
