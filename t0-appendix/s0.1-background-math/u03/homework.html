Part F — Extended Response

Q24:
(a) Speed of light: 3.00 × 10⁸ m·s⁻¹. Wavelength: 7.0 × 10⁻⁷ m.
(b) 7.0 × 10⁻⁷ m = 700 × 10⁻⁹ m = 700 nm (nanometers — the most natural prefix for visible light).
(c) Yes, both are complete. Each has a number AND a unit, so we know exactly what quantity is described and its magnitude.
Q25: SI is more convenient because all conversions between units are powers of 10 (multiply/divide by 10, 100, 1000 etc.), unlike Imperial where conversions are irregular (1 mile = 5,280 ft; 1 gallon = 128 fl oz). Example 1: 1 kilometer = 1,000 meters (just move the decimal) vs. 1 mile = 5,280 feet. Example 2: We can scale units with prefixes — 1 nanometer = 10⁻⁹ m — all using the same unit and simple powers of 10.
Q26: The student is wrong. m·s means meters multiplied by seconds (not divided). Speed is distance divided by time, which is m/s = m·s⁻¹. The negative exponent (⁻¹) on s is what creates the division — it is the IB/scientific way of writing "per second." m·s would have units of meter-seconds, which is not a standard physical quantity.
