Part E — Short Answer

Q20: "42" alone gives no information about what is being measured — it could be seconds, kilograms, or anything. Adding "meters" specifies both the type of quantity (length/distance) and the scale of measurement. A complete physical quantity always needs a number AND a unit.
Q21: The student tried to add quantities with different units (kg and m). You can only add or subtract quantities with the same unit, just as you cannot simplify x + y in algebra. The correct response is that 10 kg + 5 m cannot be simplified — it's a physically meaningless operation.
Q22: The kilogram is the only fundamental SI unit that already contains a prefix ("kilo"). All other fundamental units (meter, second, Kelvin, mole, ampere, candela) have no prefix. When adding prefixes to mass, we apply them to "gram" not "kilogram" — for example, milligram (mg), not milli-kilogram.
Q23: 4.5 km·s⁻² → km = 10³ m, so 4.5 km·s⁻² = 4.5 × 10³ m·s⁻² = 4500 m·s⁻². This is an acceleration unit (meters per second squared).
