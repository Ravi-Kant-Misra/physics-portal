Part D — Short Answer

Q17. Student A: % = (5/50)×100 = 10%. Student B: fractional = 0.1, % = 0.1×100 = 10%. Both students have the same percent uncertainty (10%). Student A wrote it as absolute (5 cm), while Student B used fractional (0.1) — but they represent identical precision.

Q18. The position of the unit signals the type of uncertainty. If the unit appears after the uncertainty (e.g. 4.00 ± 0.25 kg), the uncertainty is absolute — it has the same unit as the measurement. If the unit appears only after the number (e.g. 4.00 kg ± 0.25), the uncertainty is fractional — a pure ratio with no units. Example: "50 N ± 5 N" is absolute (uncertainty is 5 newtons); "50 N ± 0.1" is fractional (uncertainty is 10% of 50 N, expressed as 0.1 of the value).
