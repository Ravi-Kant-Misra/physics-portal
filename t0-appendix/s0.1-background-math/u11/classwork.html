Part D — Short Answer
Q15. s³ = 3.0³ = 27 cm³. Δa/a = 0.3/3.0 = 0.10. ΔV/V = 3 × 0.10 = 0.30 (30%). Absolute uncertainty = 0.30 × 27 = 8.1 cm³. Final: V = 27 ± 8 cm³ (or 27 cm³ ± 30%).
Q16. T depends on √L, so n = ½ for L. ΔL/L = 0.04/1.00 = 0.04. ΔT/T = ½ × 0.04 = 0.02 (2%). The fractional uncertainty in T is half the fractional uncertainty in L.
Q17. When you cube a measurement, you are effectively multiplying it by itself three times. Each multiplication adds the same fractional uncertainty, so the total fractional uncertainty triples. Taking a cube root reverses this: instead of multiplying by the power 3, you multiply by 1/3 — so the fractional uncertainty is reduced to one-third. In general, powers greater than 1 amplify fractional uncertainty; fractional powers (roots) reduce it.
